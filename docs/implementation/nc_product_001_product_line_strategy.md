## Executive Summary

The **NC-PRODUCT-001 Product Line Strategy** defines the commercial architecture for the Nature & Culture platform. We move beyond simple "print-on-demand" by anchoring every physical product in the platform's **Intelligence Stack**. Every item sold is a physical artifact of a verified scientific and cultural narrative, sourced from NASA, NOAA, NARA, BHL, and world-class museums.

---

## 1. Planetary Nature Collection

*   **Target Customer:** Astronomy enthusiasts, environmentalists, modern interior designers, and "Overview Effect" seekers.
*   **Hero Assets:** NASA AS08-14-2383 "Earthrise", Blue Marble (Apollo 17), Black Marble (Night Lights).
*   **Product Formats:** Large-format fine art prints (Gelato), acrylic wall art, minimalist t-shirts (limited drop).
*   **Price Tiers:** $45 (Standard Print) / $150 (Premium Framed) / $450 (Large Acrylic Master).
*   **Story Angle:** "The perspective that changed the world."
*   **Product Copy:** "1968: The year we finally saw home. This museum-grade reproduction of the original Apollo 8 Hasselblad frame is restored to its raw, breathtaking clarity."
*   **Launch Priority:** **CRITICAL (Launch Day).**
*   **Risks:** High market saturation; requires superior restoration and provenance storytelling to differentiate.
*   **Attribution:** "Image Credit: NASA / Johnson Space Center."

## 2. National Parks Collection

*   **Target Customer:** Outdoor adventurers, National Park collectors, Americana enthusiasts, and history buffs.
*   **Hero Assets:** Thomas Moran's *Yellowstone*, Hayden Expedition Maps (1871), Powell Expedition Photos (NARA).
*   **Product Formats:** Framed fine art prints, vintage-style maps on archival rag, postcards (Gelato).
*   **Price Tiers:** $15 (Postcard Pack) / $85 (Unframed Map) / $225 (Large Framed Moran).
*   **Story Angle:** "The art that convinced a nation to save itself."
*   **Product Copy:** "Before it was a park, it was a painting. Own the Thomas Moran masterpiece that convinced Congress to protect Yellowstone forever."
*   **Launch Priority:** **HIGH (Pilot Phase).**
*   **Risks:** Competes with official park gift shops; requires "archival/scientific" angle to differentiate.
*   **Attribution:** "Source: National Archives and Records Administration / Smithsonian Institution."

*   **Price Tiers:** $35 (Educational Poster) / $95 (Framed Bathymetry) / $180 (Satellite Art).
*   **Story Angle:** "The topography of the deep."

*   **Price Tiers:** $25 (Note Card Pack) / $65 (Large Print) / $120 (Collector's Field Guide).
*   **Story Angle:** "Where art meets evolution."
*   **Product Copy:** "The mechanics of mutation, captured in ink. John Gould's 1837 finch series represents the visual evidence that helped Darwin change the world."
