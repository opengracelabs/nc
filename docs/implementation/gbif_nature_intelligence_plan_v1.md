# GBIF Nature Intelligence Plan v1

**Subject:** Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF)
**Strategy:** Place-Based Intelligence & Multi-Institutional Enrichment
**Status:** Implementation Roadmap
**Date:** June 11, 2026

---

## 1. The Strategy: "Biological Truth" for Every Place

The **GBIF Nature Intelligence Plan** establishes GBIF as the "Biological Ground Truth" for the Nature & Culture platform. By integrating GBIF’s species occurrence data with the macro-scale collections of NASA, NOAA, BHL, and NARA, we create a multi-dimensional "Place Intelligence" layer. This plan transforms raw data into "Nature Intelligence"—the ability to understand exactly what life exists at a specific location, how it has changed over time, and what human stories or policies have shaped its fate.

---

## 2. Institutional Enrichment Framework

### **A. Enriching NASA: From Habitat to Residents**
*   **The Synergy:** NASA provides the **"Habitat Map"** (satellite imagery, climate data, land use); GBIF provides the **"Census"** (the species actually living there).
*   **Place Intelligence Layer:**
    *   **Climate Witness:** Overlay NASA's 30-year surface temperature heat maps with GBIF species distribution shifts. Show users how "Residents" are moving as their "House" (Habitat) warms.
    *   **Habitat Health:** Validate NASA’s NDVI (vegetation index) by showing the specific pollinators and plants recorded by GBIF in those high-health zones.
*   **Key Tool:** Google Earth Engine (GEE) integration for real-time overlay of NASA environmental layers and GBIF occurrences.

### **B. Enriching NOAA: Anchoring the "Blue" Economy**
*   **The Synergy:** NOAA provides the **"Macro View"** (underwater habitats, sanctuary boundaries, weather energy); GBIF/OBIS provides the **"Biological Identity"** (taxonomic validation and historical timelines).
*   **Place Intelligence Layer:**
    *   **Sanctuary Biodiversity:** Enrich a NOAA "Hero Shot" of a National Marine Sanctuary (e.g., *Monterey Bay*) with a 50-year biological checklist of its residents.
    *   **Ocean Currents & Migration:** Link NOAA's Gulf Stream heat maps with GBIF records of migrating species (whales, turtles, sharks) to visualize the "Highways of Life."
*   **Key Tool:** OBIS (Ocean Biodiversity Information System) nodes for marine-specific data verification.

### **C. Enriching BHL: Bridging "Then" and "Now"**
*   **The Synergy:** BHL provides the **"Cultural Story"** (historical illustrations, explorer narratives); GBIF provides the **"Scientific Proof"** (modern GPS records and validated taxonomy).
*   **Place Intelligence Layer:**
    *   **Explorer Retrospective:** In a Discovery Journey, overlay the 19th-century collection route of an explorer (from BHL field notes) with modern GBIF data. Show what species they found then vs. what remains today.
    *   **Illustrated Taxonomy:** Pair a beautiful BHL species etching with a GBIF distribution map. "The Art of the Past Meets the Science of the Present."
*   **Key Tool:** BioStor and GNA (Global Names Architecture) for linking BHL literature pages to GBIF taxon keys.

### **D. Enriching NARA: From Policy to Impact**
*   **The Synergy:** NARA provides the **"Human Footprint"** (expedition records, conservation laws, treaty boundaries); GBIF provides the **"Natural Response"** (biodiversity status within those human boundaries).
*   **Place Intelligence Layer:**
    *   **Heritage Conservation:** Link NARA records of the creation of a National Park or Sanctuary to the GBIF biodiversity trends within that area since its founding. "The Success of the Law Written in Life."
    *   **Expedition Legacy:** Use NARA’s historical photographs and maps from federal expeditions (e.g., *US Exploring Expedition*) to anchor GBIF "Deep History" occurrence records.
*   **Key Tool:** GRSciColl (Global Registry of Scientific Collections) to track the lineage of specimens from NARA-documented expeditions.

## 3. Implementation Use Cases

### **Case 1: The "Digital Interpretive Sign"**
When a user clicks on a location in the "Nature & Culture" map:
1.  **NASA:** Shows the last 10 years of forest cover change.
2.  **GBIF:** Lists the top 10 most common birds and plants recorded there.
3.  **BHL:** Provides a link to the first historical description of a species from that area.
4.  **NARA:** Shows the original 1930s federal proclamation protecting the site.

### **Case 2: The "Migration of the Monarchy"**
Tracing the Humpback Whale:
1.  **NOAA:** Satellite tracking and sanctuary boundaries in Stellwagen Bank.
2.  **GBIF:** 20 years of occurrence data along the migration route.
3.  **NARA:** Historical whaling records showing the decline before the 1972 protection act.
4.  **BHL:** 19th-century scientific drawings of whale anatomy.

## 4. Operational Roadmap

1.  **Taxonomic Indexing:** Use the GBIF Taxonomic Backbone as the universal key to link assets across all four institutions.
2.  **Spatial Indexing:** Use GBIF’s georeferenced records to create "Place-Based Clusters" that automatically surface related NOAA, NASA, and NARA assets.
3.  **Discovery Service:** Deploy a "GBIF Intelligence Widget" on all discovery pages that provides real-time biodiversity stats for any place or species mentioned in the narrative.
