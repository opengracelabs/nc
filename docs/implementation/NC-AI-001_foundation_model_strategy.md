## 1. AI Product Vision: "The Intelligence of Synthesis"

At Nature & Culture, Artificial Intelligence is not a replacement for human discovery or source truth. Instead, foundation models serve as the **Synthesizer**—the bridge between massive, siloed institutional archives and the human sense of wonder. 

Our vision is to use AI to:
1.  **Scale Multilingualism:** Bridge the language gap for global heritage.
2.  **Uncover Connections:** Identify links between species, places, and history that are hidden in plain sight.
3.  **Personalize Discovery:** Adapt complex scientific data for different audiences (children, researchers, travelers).
4.  **Protect Integrity:** Use models to audit and verify rights, provenance, and attribution at scale.

We adhere to the **UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence** and the **UN Global Digital Compact**, ensuring that our AI implementation is transparent, inclusive, and grounded in human rights and environmental sustainability.

## 3. Open-Source Model Strategy

Nature & Culture prioritizes an **Open-First Architecture**. While frontier models provide the "ceiling" of our capabilities, open-source models (Mistral, Gemma, Qwen) provide the "floor" of our sustainability and sovereignty.

*   **Self-Hosting:** We aim to host small, specialized models for sensitive rights-checking tasks to ensure data privacy and system resilience.
*   **Contribution:** NC will contribute "Grounding Adapters" to the open-source community, sharing our methods for grounding LLMs in public-domain scientific records.

## 4. Feature Roadmap

### 4.1 Website AI Features
*   **Grounded Search:** A search bar that returns not just links, but a synthesized answer citing specific BHL/NASA records.
*   **Semantic Explorer:** Interactive visual graphs showing connections between "Place A" and "Species B" based on archive evidence.

### 4.2 Product Generation Features
*   **Provenance Remastering:** AI-assisted restoration of 19th-century lithographs (denoising, color recovery) while maintaining historical accuracy.
*   **Custom Heritage Prints:** Allowing users to generate custom "Intelligence Overlays" (e.g., thermal data over a landscape painting) for unique products.

### 4.3 Place Intelligence Features
*   **Live Resilience Tracking:** Synthesizing satellite data and field notes to create "Status Reports" for pilot places (e.g., GBR bleaching alerts).
*   **The "Deep Time" Slider:** Using generative visuals to show how a landscape (like the Grand Canyon) evolved over millions of years, grounded in geological data.

### 4.4 Education Features
*   **Adaptive Lesson Packs:** AI-generated teacher guides that adjust scientific complexity based on grade level, using the same source evidence.
*   **Citizen Science Copilot:** Helping students identify species or geological features in the field by comparing their photos to NC-verified archives.

### 4.5 Tourism Features
*   **The Ethical Companion:** An AI travel guide that provides "Respectful Visit" protocols based on UNESCO/Local governance.
*   **The Narrative Audio-Guide:** Dynamic, location-aware audio stories that change based on current time/weather (e.g., a "Rainy Day in Venice" history).

### 4.6 Multilingual Features
*   **Universal Access:** Real-time, grounded translation of archival records into 50+ languages, prioritizing the 8 core NC languages (EN, FR, ES, DE, PT, ZH, JA, ID).

## 5. Future Concept: The "Voice of Wonder" Assistant

A conversational interface that does not "hallucinate" but "recalls." Users can ask: *"What did John Muir think of the granite in Yosemite?"* or *"Show me the stars over Yellowstone on the night the park was founded."* The assistant responds with direct quotes, images, and data visualizations, always citing the source.

## 7. Hard Governance Rules (The Invariants)

1.  **Grounding Mandatory:** Every AI output must cite at least one verified source record (NASA, BHL, etc.).
2.  **Rights Neutrality:** AI may never declare an asset "Public Domain" or "Rights-Safe." It may only surface existing evidence for human ruling.
3.  **Non-Endorsement:** No AI output may imply that NASA, NOAA, or any partner institution "endorses" or "vets" Nature & Culture products.
4.  **No Deferred Assets:** AI workers are prohibited from using assets marked as "Deferred" in the central registry.
5.  **Human-in-the-Loop:** All public-facing AI-generated copy must pass a human "Narrative Review" before publication.
