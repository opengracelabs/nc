# NC-WEB-001 Website Wireframe + Creative Direction

## Project Overview
**Brand:** Nature & Culture
**Tagline:** The Intelligence of Wonder
**Objective:** Establish the first digital flagship for Nature & Culture, focusing on high-authority public-domain heritage and planetary conservation.

---

## 1. Visual Style Direction: "Scientific Fine Art"

### 1.1 Core Aesthetic
A fusion of 19th-century scientific observation and 21st-century digital minimalism. The site should feel like a "Living Archive"—authoritative and rugged, yet elegant and high-tech.

### 1.2 Color Palette
*   **Deep Space (#0A0A0B):** Primary background for NASA/Orbital content.
*   **Heritage Parchment (#F4F1EA):** Primary background for archival/illustration content.
*   **Earth Horizon (#2B4C7E):** Primary accent blue.
*   **Wilderness Green (#3D5245):** Primary accent green.
*   **Obsidian Type (#1A1A1A):** Main text color.

### 1.3 Typography
*   **Header:** *Spectral* or similar high-contrast Serif (Authoritative, Academic, Timeless).
*   **Body:** *Inter* or similar clean Sans-Serif (Modern, Accessible, Intelligent).
*   **Monospace:** *JetBrains Mono* for metadata and technical specs (Precision, Archive).

## 9. First User Journey: "The Overview Effect"

1.  **Entry:** User arrives via an Instagram ad featuring the "Blue Marble" and the tagline "The Intelligence of Wonder."
2.  **Home:** User scrolls down the homepage, seeing the 7 pilot places. They are intrigued by "Earthrise" listed as a place.
3.  **Place Page:** They click Earthrise. They see the immersive story of the Apollo 8 mission and the "Oasis in Space."
4.  **Story:** They read about the "Overview Effect" and see the technical NASA metadata. They feel a sense of "Reverence."
5.  **Conversion:** At the bottom of the story, they see "Bring the Moment Home." They click the Earthrise Heritage Print.
6.  **Shop:** They configure a 24x36 print with an Obsidian frame.
7.  **Exit:** User purchases the print, feeling they have bought not just art, but a piece of conservation history.
