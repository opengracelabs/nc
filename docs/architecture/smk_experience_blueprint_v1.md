# SMK Experience Blueprint v1: Stillness and Light

**Asset:** *Interior in Strandgade, Sunlight on the Floor* (1901)  
**Object Number:** KMS3696  
**Institution:** Statens Museum for Kunst (SMK)  
**Focus:** Denmark · Copenhagen · Nordic Light · Silence · Interior Space

---

## 1. Wonder: The Homepage Hero ("The Silent Threshold")

**Goal:** Induce a state of contemplative calm through the mastery of Nordic light.

*   **Experience:** Full-screen, minimalist entry. A subtle **"Light Drift" WebGL shader** animates the sunlight on the floor, making it slowly crawl across the woodgrain as if time itself is slowing down.
*   **Visual:** The high-resolution crop focuses on the interface between the doorframe and the pool of light.
*   **Audio:** "The Sound of Silence"—a binaural recording of a quiet room in Copenhagen: the distant, muffled ring of a church bell (Our Saviour's Church), a floorboard creak, and the soft whistle of wind against glass.
*   **Interaction:** A "Breathe In" button that gradually fades the UI, leaving only the painting and the light drift effect.

## 2. Trust: The National Archive

**Goal:** Establish the SMK as the definitive guardian of Danish cultural identity.

*   **Provenance Layer:** An interactive timeline showing the work's journey from Hammershøi's home at **Strandgade 30** to the SMK National Gallery in 1924.
*   **Institutional Seal:** The **SMK Open** verified badge, providing direct transparency to the CC0 metadata and high-resolution TIFF master via IIIF.
*   **Technical Proof:** A "Pigment Analysis" overlay describing Hammershøi's restricted palette (Greys, Whites, Ochres) and how he achieved "Vibration" without vibrant color.

## 3. Connection: The Place Page ("Strandgade 30")

**Goal:** Anchor the interior "Soul" of the painting in the physical "Body" of Copenhagen.

*   **Geographic Anchor:** **Strandgade 30**, Christianshavn (Copenhagen).
*   **Virtual Window:** A split-screen interaction:
    *   *Left:* Hammershøi's 1901 painting of the room.
    *   *Right:* A modern photograph of the same architectural space in Strandgade, demonstrating the timelessness of the Danish interior.
*   **The Nordic Light Engine:** A real-time data visualization of the sun's angle in Copenhagen, showing the exact moments of the year when the "Hammershøi Light" appears in Christianshavn.
*   **Contextual Discovery:** A guided walk through **Christianshavn**, linking the painting to the canals, the cobblestones, and the unique architecture of the district.

## 4. Discovery: The Media Viewer ("The Minimalist Lens")

**Goal:** Reveal the hidden textures and "Dust Motes" of the Hammershøi aesthetic.

*   **Interaction:** 2500% IIIF Deep-Zoom into the floorboards and the door handle.
*   **Guided Hotspots:**
    *   **The Vibration of Grey:** Explaining how Hammershøi applied thin layers of paint to create a surface that seems to hum with energy.
    *   **The Absent Figure:** Discussing the intentional choice of the "Empty Room" as a modern psychological portrait.
    *   **The Woodgrain:** Highlighting the extreme detail in the floor, where the sunlight reveals the physical history of the house.

## 5. Commerce: The Nordic Boutique ("The Strandgade Collection")

**Goal:** Enable visitors to bring the "Hygge" of Nordic minimalism into their own homes.

*   **Curated Products:**
    *   **Masterwork Prints:** Giclée reproductions on matte, archival paper to preserve the velvety greys.
    *   **"Sunlight & Cedar" Candle:** A scent profile inspired by the painting: Aged Wood, Cold Air, and Dust in Sunbeams.
    *   **The Minimalist Stationery Set:** High-quality paper in the Hammershøi palette (Charcoal, Pearl, Bone).
*   **CTA:** "Support the SMK National Gallery with every acquisition from the Hammershøi archive."
