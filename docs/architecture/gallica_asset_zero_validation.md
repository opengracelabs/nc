## 2. Recommendation Rationale

The **Catalan Atlas** is the definitive "Genesis Asset" for Gallica. While Redouté's roses offer unmatched commercial potential for prints, they lack the deep, place-centered "Discovery" narrative required for an Asset Zero. The Catalan Atlas is not merely a map; it is a medieval encyclopedia of the known world, representing the pivot point where geography, commerce, and mythology converged.

### Why it was chosen:
*   **The "Mansa Musa" Narrative:** It contains the most famous medieval depiction of Mansa Musa, King of Mali, holding a gold nugget. This provides an immediate, high-authority anchor for African heritage and global trade history.
*   **Technical Benchmarking:** The asset consists of six vellum leaves with extreme detail (gold leaf, intricate calligraphy, and miniature illustrations). It is the perfect stress test for the **Universal Media Substrate’s** IIIF "Infinite Zoom" capabilities.
*   **Visual Authority:** It establishes Gallica as the premier source for "Wonder" assets, transcending simple natural history into the realm of rare, high-prestige manuscripts.

## 3. The Story: "The Golden King and the Silk Road"

In 1375, on the island of Majorca, the cartographer Abraham Cresques compiled the sum of medieval knowledge onto six leaves of vellum for King Charles V of France. The result was the Catalan Atlas. 

The "Story" of this asset centers on the **Mansa Musa panel**. As the user zooms into the West African interior, they encounter the King of Mali seated on a throne, holding a golden orb. This is the first time a European map acknowledged the extreme wealth and sophistication of the African interior. By linking this artistic representation to modern geological data on West African gold deposits and the historical Silk Road trade routes, Nature & Culture transforms a 14th-century map into a living portal of economic and cultural history.
