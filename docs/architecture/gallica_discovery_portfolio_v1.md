# Gallica Discovery Portfolio v1

**Role:** Chief Curator  
**Objective:** Identify the first 50 high-impact, rights-cleared assets to ingest via the Gallica adapter, expanding Nature & Culture's reach into the Francophone world and the French colonial legacy.

---

## 1. Portfolio Strategy: The "Imperial Enlightenment"

This portfolio prioritizes assets that document the French contribution to global science, cartography, and exploration. We focus on Gallica’s unique strengths: the "Golden Age" of botanical illustration (Redouté), the encyclopedic natural history of Buffon, and the definitive mapping of the French colonial empire (Indochina, West Africa, New France).

### Ranking Criteria:
1.  **Visual Authority:** Is it a definitive representation of its genre or period?
2.  **French Heritage:** Does it highlight the specific strengths of the BnF collections?
3.  **Place Relevance:** Does it fill geographic gaps in North Africa, SE Asia, and the Pacific?
4.  **Commercial Value:** Is the aesthetic suitable for high-end commerce (botanical prints, historic maps)?

---

## 2. Recommended Asset Zero: The "Genesis Asset"

### **The Catalan Atlas (1375)**
*   **Asset:** *Atlas de cartes célestes et de cartes de navigation* (The Catalan Atlas), attributed to Abraham Cresques.
*   **Creator:** Abraham Cresques (Majorcan Cartographic School).
*   **Institution:** Bibliothèque nationale de France / Gallica.
*   **Rights:** Public Domain Mark.
*   **Reason for Selection:** This is the most important map of the medieval period. It represents a pivot point where geography, mythology, and commerce intersect. Its vibrant colors, gold leaf, and depiction of figures like Mansa Musa make it a world-class "wonder" asset.
*   **Recommended Use:** The core visual anchor for the **Global Discovery Path**. Validates the "Wonder" pillar and showcases the platform's ability to handle intricate, large-scale historic manuscripts.
