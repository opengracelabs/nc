# Gallica Retirement Impact Assessment

**Objective:** Evaluate the coverage lost by removing Bibliothèque nationale de France (BnF) / Gallica from production sources and recommend a replacement strategy across ranked institutions.

---

## 1. Coverage Lost by Removing Gallica

Removing Gallica creates significant gaps in the Nature & Culture platform's coverage, specifically impacting the Francophone world and the "Golden Age" of illustration.

*   **France & Paris:** Loss of the primary institutional home for French national heritage. While Paris Musées covers municipal collections, Gallica provides the definitive national-level historical archive for France.
*   **Natural History & Illustration:** Critical loss of the "Golden Age" of French botanical and zoological illustration. This includes the massive *Histoire Naturelle* by Georges-Louis Buffon (tens of thousands of plates) and the definitive original plates of Pierre-Joseph Redouté (*Les Roses*, *Les Liliacées*).
*   **Maps:** Loss of the definitive cartographic record of the French colonial empire, including high-value historical maps of New France (North America), French Indochina, West Africa, and the Indian Ocean.
*   **Tourism & Commerce:** Loss of the primary "Gateway Assets" for high-UNESCO value Francophone locations. For example, Gallica was the sole source for Madagascar's endemic fauna (lemurs/fossas via Buffon and Sonnerat) and early mapping, as well as crucial historical views for Angkor Wat (Cambodia).

---

## 2. Replacement Strategy & Rankings

To mitigate these losses, the following institutions must be prioritized to fill the geographic, biological, and commercial gaps.

### 1. Trove (National Library of Australia)
*   **Role:** Pacific, Indian Ocean & Colonial Geography Replacement.
*   **Justification:** Trove is essential to offset the loss of Gallica's French colonial mapping. It provides unparalleled coverage of Oceania, early maritime exploration, and the broader Asia-Pacific region, filling the geographic gaps left in Indochina, Polynesia, and historical tourism mapping.

### 2. NHM London (Natural History Museum)
*   **Role:** Natural History & Illustration Replacement.
*   **Justification:** NHM London is the mandatory 1:1 replacement for the loss of Buffon and Redouté. Holding 80 million specimens and exceptional "Golden Age" illustrations (e.g., the Rothschild Collection of tropical birds/insects and the Walter Collection), NHM completely restores the platform's biological illustration and commercial print pipeline.

### 3. NARA (National Archives and Records Administration)
*   **Role:** Global Maps & Historical Photography Supplement.
*   **Justification:** NARA provides massive volumetric scale for historical photography and government cartography. While US-centric, its global military and diplomatic archives provide sufficient mapping and photographic coverage to offset broad geographic discovery gaps.

### 4. Other Candidates
*   **Paris Musées (Direct France/Paris Replacement):** Since Gallica is retired, Paris Musées (already authorized via DD-PARISMUSEES-001) must be elevated to handle 100% of the **France** and **Paris** geographic intelligence. Musée Carnavalet fully replaces Gallica for Paris history, while the Petit Palais anchors French Impressionism.
*   **MNHN Paris (Muséum national d'Histoire naturelle):** If strict French-origin natural history is required for specific commercial lines, MNHN Paris is the direct institutional substitute for Gallica's biological archives.
