# Met Discovery Portfolio v1

**Role:** Chief Curator  
**Objective:** Identify the first 50 high-impact, rights-cleared assets to ingest via the Metropolitan Museum of Art (The Met) adapter, activating the Japan geographic gap and strengthening NC's natural history and archaeology coverage.

---

## 1. Portfolio Strategy: The "Encyclopedic Horizon"

This portfolio prioritizes assets that bridge the gap between Art and Nature, specifically focusing on The Met's "Open Access" (CC0) collection. We focus on the Japan geographic gap (ukiyo-e), foundational natural history illustrations (Merian, Dürer, Audubon), and iconic archaeology (Egypt).

### Ranking Criteria:
1.  **Commercial Activation:** Does the asset have strong potential for high-end commerce (prints, wallpaper, textiles)?
2.  **Japan Gap Closure:** Does it associate with priority Japanese place pages (Fuji, Edo, Kyoto)?
3.  **Institutional Authority:** Does it represent the Met's most iconic and unique CC0 holdings?
4.  **Taxonomic Value:** Can the asset be used to illustrate biological taxa (botanical/zoological)?

---

## 2. Recommended Asset Zero: The "Masterwork Pivot"

### **Under the Wave off Kanagawa (The Great Wave)**
*   **Asset:** *Under the Wave off Kanagawa* (Kanagawa oki nami ura), from the series *Thirty-Six Views of Mount Fuji*.
*   **Creator:** Katsushika Hokusai (Japanese, 1760–1849).
*   **Institution:** The Metropolitan Museum of Art.
*   **Rights:** CC0 (Open Access).
*   **Reason for Selection:** The "Great Wave" is arguably the most famous Japanese work of art in the world. It serves as the perfect "Asset Zero" for The Met, validating the ukiyo-e pipeline and providing an iconic visual for the Japan place intelligence mission.
*   **Recommended Use:** The primary visual anchor for **Japan Place Pages**. Validates the "Masterwork" commerce tier and demonstrates the platform's ability to handle high-resolution, culturally significant ukiyo-e prints.
