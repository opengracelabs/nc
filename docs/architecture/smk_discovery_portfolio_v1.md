# SMK Discovery Portfolio v1

**Role:** Chief Curator  
**Objective:** Identify the first 50 high-impact, rights-cleared assets from the Statens Museum for Kunst (SMK - National Gallery of Denmark), activating the Scandinavia geographic gap and strengthening NC's Nordic landscape and exploration coverage.

---

## 1. Portfolio Strategy: The "Nordic Horizon"

This portfolio prioritizes assets that define the unique atmosphere, light, and history of the Nordic region. We focus on the SMK's world-class collection of Danish Golden Age painting (Danish identity), Nordic landscapes (Norway, Sweden, Denmark), and ethnographic/exploration records from Greenland and the Arctic.

### Ranking Criteria:
1.  **Atmospheric Authority:** Does the asset capture the unique "Nordic Light" or climate?
2.  **Geographic Gap Closure:** Does it associate with priority Nordic place pages (Copenhagen, Jutland, Greenland)?
3.  **Commercial Resonance:** Is the aesthetic suitable for high-end commerce (Minimalist/Scandi-style prints)?
4.  **Scientific/Historical Value:** Does it document natural history (Flora Danica) or exploration (Arctic)?

---

## 2. Recommended Asset Zero: The "Luminous Threshold"

### **Dust Motes Dancing in the Sunbeams**
*   **Asset:** *Dust Motes Dancing in the Sunbeams* (Solstråler eller Støvkornenes dans i solstrålerne)
*   **Creator:** Vilhelm Hammershøi (Danish, 1864–1916).
*   **Institution:** Statens Museum for Kunst (SMK).
*   **Rights:** CC0 (Open Access).
*   **Reason for Selection:** This is Hammershøi's most famous work and a global icon of Nordic interior painting. It perfectly exemplifies the "Wonder" pillar through its evocative treatment of light and silence. It serves as the ideal "Asset Zero" to validate the SMK pipeline and provide a platform-defining visual for the Nordic mission.
*   **Recommended Use:** The primary visual anchor for **Copenhagen / Denmark Place Pages**. Validates the "Masterwork" commerce tier and demonstrates the platform's ability to handle high-fidelity, atmospheric light rendering.
