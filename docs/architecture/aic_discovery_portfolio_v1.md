# AIC Discovery Portfolio v1

**Role:** Chief Curator  
**Objective:** Identify the first 50 high-impact, rights-cleared assets to ingest from the Art Institute of Chicago (AIC), strengthening NC's French Impressionism, Japanese prints, and global geographic coverage.

---

## 1. Portfolio Strategy: The "Modernist Horizon"

This portfolio prioritizes assets that document the emergence of the modern worldview, from the light-drenched parks of Paris to the woodblock-printed streets of Edo. We focus on the AIC's unique strengths: French Impressionism (Paris/France place pages), Japanese ukiyo-e (Japan gap), and a strong foundational collection of photography and ethnographic art (Africa, Pacific).

### Ranking Criteria:
1.  **Visual Authority:** Is it a definitive representation of its genre (e.g., Pointillism, Ukiyo-e)?
2.  **Geographic Gap Closure:** Does it associate with priority place pages (France, Japan, Africa)?
3.  **Commercial Value:** Is the aesthetic suitable for high-end commerce (Impressionist prints, photography)?
4.  **Taxonomic Value:** Can the asset illustrate biological taxa (botanical/zoological)?

---

## 2. Recommended Asset Zero: The "Pointillist Anchor"

### **A Sunday on La Grande Jatte — 1884**
*   **Asset:** *A Sunday on La Grande Jatte — 1884*
*   **Creator:** Georges Seurat (French, 1859–1891).
*   **Institution:** Art Institute of Chicago.
*   **Rights:** CC0 (Open Access).
*   **Reason for Selection:** This is one of the most famous paintings in the world and the centerpiece of the AIC's collection. It serves as the perfect "Asset Zero" for the AIC, validating the French Impressionism pipeline and providing an iconic visual for the France/Paris place intelligence mission.
*   **Recommended Use:** The primary visual anchor for **France/Paris Place Pages**. Validates the "Masterwork" commerce tier and demonstrates the platform's ability to handle complex, world-renowned fine art.
