## Governing Method

This document audits the 98 unique places in NC-PLACES-001 against seven international
heritage and conservation designation systems. The audit identifies structural gaps —
designations, regions, cultures, and ecosystems absent from the current candidate set —
and produces a batched roadmap from 100 to 2,000 places.

**The audit is comparative, not additive.** It does not create a new taxonomy. It applies
the NC-PLACES-1000 taxonomy to identify where the current set is systematically thin.

**Audit criteria:** Each of the seven designation systems is treated as an independent
coverage lens. A gap is structural when an entire class of places — a designation tier,
a region, a culture, an ecosystem type — has zero or near-zero representation.

### 1.6 Dark Sky Places

**Total IDA-designated places globally:** ~200+ (Parks, Reserves, Sanctuaries, Communities)

**Coverage in NC-PLACES-001:** 2 places — Death Valley (#35) and Big Bend (#36).
Both included for NP status; Dark Sky designation is secondary.

**Missing Dark Sky classes:**

| Class | Missing places | PD connection |
|---|---|---|
| **Dark Sky Sanctuary** (most remote) | NamibRand NR (Namibia), Gabriela Mistral Reserve (Chile), Cosmic Campground (USA), Westhavelland (Germany) | Africa Dark Sky gap; Atacama science connection |
| **Dark Sky Reserve** | Aoraki Mackenzie (NZ), Kerry (Ireland), Galloway (Scotland), Mont-Mégantic (Canada) | Cook voyage South Island connection; Scottish natural history |
| **Dark Sky Park** | Natural Bridges NM (first, USA), Jasper NP (world's largest, Canada), Iriomote-Ishigaki (Japan), Zselic (Hungary) | American NP illustration; Japanese island illustration |
| **Dark Sky Island** | Sark (UK), Coll (Scotland), Niue (Pacific) | Pacific gap; island illustration |
| **Southern Hemisphere anchors** | No Southern Hemisphere Dark Sky places at all | Atacama (#57 in list) not classified as Dark Sky; Aoraki absent |

**Critical structural gap:** The X.DS (Dark Sky) domain from NC-PLACES-1000 has two places at
Tier 2 and ZERO outside North America. The Dark Sky designation uniquely connects scientific
illustration (astronomical atlases, celestial charts, navigation maps) to natural places. This is
the only path to CMF-MAP (Cartographic) products based on *natural* landscapes.

**Atacama anomaly:** Atacama Desert (#57) is in the list but not tagged as Dark Sky. It hosts
the ALMA Observatory and Paranal Observatory — the most important astronomical sites in the
Southern Hemisphere — and has the clearest night skies on Earth. It should be reclassified to
X.DS.RV (Dark Sky Reserve equivalent) or M.MX.NC (Natural-Cosmic Mixed).

### 4.2 Seven Ecosystem Types Absent from NC-PLACES-001

**1. Eurasian Steppe (tallgrass and feathergrass)**
- Area: ~8.5 million km² — world's largest terrestrial biome by area
- Status: ZERO places
- Missing: Kazakh Steppe (Saryarka, UNESCO WH Natural), Mongolian Steppe (Orkhon Valley, UNESCO WH Mixed), Kalahari/Highveld transition
- PD illustration: Pallas's Siberian/Central Asian expedition illustrations; Russian Imperial botanical surveys; Eurasian steppe birds (bustards, cranes, raptors)
- Institution: Europeana (Pallas, Güldenstädt expedition plates)

**2. Boreal Forest / Taiga**
- Area: ~11.5 million km² — world's largest forest biome
- Status: Boundary Waters and Białowieża touch the boreal edge; NO dedicated taiga site
- Missing: Wood Buffalo NP (Canada, UNESCO WH Natural + Ramsar — whooping crane + bison), Putorana Plateau (Russia, UNESCO WH), Oulanka NP (Finland)
- PD illustration: Russian Imperial taiga expedition records; NHM boreal species illustration
- Critical place: `wood-buffalo-national-park` — the only breeding ground of the wild whooping crane. Audubon-adjacent illustration anchor.

**3. Seagrass and Shallow Reef System**
- Most diverse marine habitat after coral reefs
- Status: ZERO dedicated places
- Missing: Shark Bay (Australia, UNESCO WH Natural + Ramsar — world's largest seagrass + stromatolites), Banc d'Arguin (Mauritania, UNESCO WH Natural + Ramsar)
- PD illustration: NHM seagrass and shallow reef natural history; NOAA coastal surveys
- Critical place: `shark-bay` — the world's most diverse seagrass ecosystem and home to stromatolites (3.5-billion-year-old microbial mats). Darwin/Dampier connection.

**4. Freshwater Megarivers**
- World's great rivers as conservation and cultural landscapes
- Status: Amazon NP (partially); no dedicated river-as-place anywhere else
- Missing: Congo River Basin (DRC/Congo — world's deepest, second largest discharge), Mekong River (UNESCO tentative WH, Living Mekong Initiative), Irrawaddy, Ganges/Brahmaputra
- PD illustration: Livingstone expedition Congo/Zambezi records; Wallace Malay rivers; Humboldt Orinoco; Cook voyage rivers
- Critical: `mekong-river-corridor` — combines Mekong ICH (Traditional weaving, boat racing festivals), Irrawaddy dolphin, and Mekong giant catfish. Southeast Asian gap fill.

**5. Atlantic and Indian Ocean Atolls**
- Isolated coral ring-reef systems; among Earth's most biodiverse marine habitats
- Status: Papahānaumokuākea (Pacific), GBR (continental shelf), Palau — all Pacific. ZERO Atlantic or Indian Ocean atolls.
- Missing: Aldabra (Seychelles, UNESCO WH Natural), Chagos (BIOT, largest tropical marine reserve), Bijagós (Guinea-Bissau, UNESCO Biosphere), Fernando de Noronha (Brazil, UNESCO WH Natural)
- PD illustration: Cook/Baudin Indian Ocean voyage; NHM atoll expedition records

**6. Volcanic Island Chain (Atlantic arc)**
- Active volcanism + island biogeography
- Status: Hawaii (✓), Galápagos (✓), Canary Islands (absent), Azores (absent), Cape Verde (absent)
- Missing: Azores (UNESCO Geopark + Biosphere; Banks/Cook Atlantic stop), Canary Islands (Teide, UNESCO WH Natural), Réunion (WH Natural + Piton de la Fournaise), Cape Verde (sea turtle nesting + Darwin connection)
- PD illustration: Banks/Solander Azores records; Darwin volcanic geology sketches; Cook Atlantic stops

**7. Polar and Sub-Polar Islands (non-Antarctic)**
- Status: Svalbard (✓), Antarctic Peninsula (✓) — NO sub-Antarctic islands
- Missing: South Georgia + South Sandwich Islands (MPA + Heroic Age expedition anchor), Falkland Islands (Beagle voyage + Wilson/Scott connection), Crozet Islands (French sub-Antarctic), South Shetland Islands
- PD illustration: Wilson sub-Antarctic watercolours; FitzRoy/Darwin Falklands survey; Cook voyage South Georgia (1775)
- Critical: `south-georgia-island` — Edward Wilson's penguin plates + Shackleton's crossing + Challenger expedition anchor. One of the most illustration-rich sub-Antarctic places.
