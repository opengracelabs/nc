# DPLA Discovery Portfolio v1

**Role:** Chief Curator  
**Objective:** Identify the first 50 high-impact, rights-cleared assets to ingest via the DPLA adapter, establishing Nature & Culture's US-focused "Visual Authority."

---

## 1. Portfolio Strategy: The "American Frontier"

This portfolio prioritizes assets that document the intersection of North American geography, scientific discovery, and cultural heritage. We focus on DPLA’s unique strengths: WPA design, early exploration records, and the foundational photography of the American West.

### Ranking Criteria:
1.  **Visual Authority:** Is it an iconic representation of the category?
2.  **Rights Clarity:** Must be Public Domain or NoC-US.
3.  **Multimodal Potential:** Can it anchor visual, historical, and scientific narratives?
4.  **Commercial Resonance:** Does it translate well to high-end gallery prints or digital experiences?

---

## 2. Recommended Asset Zero: The "Genesis Asset"

### **John James Audubon's "Wild Turkey" (Plate 1)**
*   **Asset:** *Wild Turkey (Meleagris gallopavo)*, Plate 1 from *The Birds of America* (Double Elephant Folio).
*   **Creator:** John James Audubon.
*   **Institution:** New York Historical Society / DPLA.
*   **Rights:** Public Domain Mark.
*   **Reason for Selection:** This is the most iconic American natural history illustration ever produced. It represents the pinnacle of early 19th-century scientific observation and artistic mastery.
*   **Recommended Use:** The core visual anchor for the **NC Discovery Experience**. Validates high-fidelity IIIF zoom and sets the "Scientific Sublime" tone for the entire platform.
