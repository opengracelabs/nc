# Place-Centered Media Journey v1

**Role:** Chief Curator  
**Objective:** Define a unified, narrative-driven media journey for four iconic locations, demonstrating how diverse assets create a cohesive atmosphere and story of place.

---

## 1. Yellowstone: The Living Frontier
**Atmosphere:** Primeval, Volatile, Grand  
**Core Media:** Hayden Survey (1871)

*   **Map:** **Henry Elliott’s 1871 Lake Map.** The user enters through the first scientific rendering of the "wonderland."
*   **Photography:** **William Henry Jackson.** Stark, mammoth-plate views of Old Faithful that first proved the park's existence to the world.
*   **Botanical Art:** **Thomas Moran’s Field Sketches.** Delicate watercolors of geyserite and thermal flora found nowhere else on earth.
*   **Film:** **The "Geyser Spectacle."** Edge-to-edge, high-speed footage of the Grand Prismatic Spring, showing the "Living" nature of the earth.
*   **Audio:** **Thermal Field Recordings.** The binaural sounds of bubbling mud pots and the thunderous roar of a geyser eruption.
*   **Book:** **Nathaniel Langford’s *Diary of the Washburn Expedition*.** First-person accounts of the moment of discovery, read alongside the map.
*   **Commerce:** **The "Hayden Portfolio."** High-fidelity reprints of the 1871 survey photos and maps, funding modern geothermal preservation.

---

## 2. Yosemite: The Temple of Light
**Atmosphere:** Ethereal, Vertical, Serene  
**Core Media:** The Muir-Watkins Era

*   **Map:** **Josiah Whitney’s 1863 Survey.** A hand-drawn, vertical-relief map that emphasizes the valley's sheer granite walls.
*   **Photography:** **Carleton Watkins.** High-contrast, mammoth-plate images that capture the "Divine Light" hitting El Capitan.
*   **Botanical Art:** **John Muir’s Journals.** Rough, soulful sketches of Giant Sequoias and alpine wildflowers, integrated into the scrollytelling.
*   **Film:** **"The High Sierra."** Cinematic drone sweeps over Glacier Point at sunrise, mimicking Muir’s eagle-eye descriptions.
*   **Audio:** **"The Sound of Water."** Spatial audio of Yosemite Falls at peak runoff, transitioning to the silence of a snow-covered valley.
*   **Book:** **Muir’s *My First Summer in the Sierra*.** The poetic soul of the park, used as the primary narrative voice for the journey.
*   **Commerce:** **The "Muir Studio."** A curated collection of Muir’s sketches and Watkins’ plates, supporting Sequoia reforestation.

---

## 3. Galápagos: The Darwinian Forge
**Atmosphere:** Primordial, Scientific, Isolated  
**Core Media:** The Voyage of the Beagle (1835)

*   **Map:** **James Colnett’s 1798 Chart.** A weathered, hand-colored nautical chart used by early explorers and whalers.
*   **Photography:** **Modern "Micro-Macro" Photography.** Close-ups of the scales of an Iguana and the eye of a Tortoise, rewarding the "Close Look."
*   **Botanical Art:** **Darwin’s Beagle Sketches.** Finches, cacti, and tortoises as they appeared in Darwin’s own field notebooks.
*   **Film:** **"Under the Forge."** Immersive underwater footage of marine iguanas feeding, bringing the "Invisible" world of the islands to life.
*   **Audio:** **"The Beagle’s Deck."** The sound of creaking timber and Pacific waves, interspersed with the calls of unique island birds.
*   **Book:** **Charles Darwin’s *Voyage of the Beagle*.** The intellectual foundation of the journey, revealing the "Process" of evolution.
*   **Commerce:** **"The Darwin Archive."** Replicas of Darwin’s sketches and Colnett’s maps, funding the Charles Darwin Foundation.

---

## 4. Great Barrier Reef: The Marine Masterpiece
**Atmosphere:** Vibrant, Fragile, Infinite  
**Core Media:** Saville-Kent’s Marine Archive (1893)

*   **Map:** **19th Century Admiralty Charts.** Intricate, hand-inked maps of the Coral Sea, marking the "Great Barrier."
*   **Photography:** **William Saville-Kent.** The world’s first "underwater" style photos (from 1893), showing the reef's historical health.
*   **Botanical Art:** **Saville-Kent’s Color Lithographs.** Stunningly detailed plates of anemones, corals, and nudibranchs.
*   **Film:** **"The Infinite Reef."** Wide-angle, 360-degree footage of the spawning reef, emphasizing the scale of the ecosystem.
*   **Audio:** **"The Living Reef."** Hydrophone recordings of the reef "clicking" and "snapping"—the sound of a healthy, biodiverse environment.
*   **Book:** **Saville-Kent’s *The Great Barrier Reef*.** The definitive historical record of the reef, providing the "Authority" for the experience.
*   **Commerce:** **"The Marine Portfolio."** High-resolution lithograph prints and coral-taxonomy datasets, supporting reef restoration efforts.

---

## 5. Summary of Path Integration

| Path Domain | Integration Strategy |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Discovery Path** | Start at the **Map**. Zoom into a specific point to reveal a **Photo**. The photo triggers an **Audio** soundscape. |
| **Story Path** | The **Book** (Journals) provides the narrative voice, guiding the user through the **Map** and **Film** sequences. |
| **Education Path** | **Botanical Art** and **Datasets** provide the detailed, scientific investigation layers (The "Close Look"). |
| **Tourism Path** | The **Film** and **Audio** create "Presence," inspiring mindful visitation of the physical site. |
| **Commerce Path** | The "Portfolio" model allows users to purchase **Photography** and **Art** replicas as a form of **Patronage**. |
