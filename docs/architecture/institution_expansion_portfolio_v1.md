# Institution Expansion Portfolio v1

**Role:** Chief Curator  
**Objective:** Recommend the next three institutional partners to achieve near-complete global coverage and deliver a world-class, place-centered experience.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

To build upon the foundation of BHL, LOC, and Smithsonian, Nature & Culture must prioritize partners that fill **Regional Gaps** (Japan, Australia) and provide **Visual Excellence** (High-fidelity CC0). The following three institutions are recommended for immediate integration.

---

## 2. Recommended Institutions

### I. The J. Paul Getty Museum (Getty)
*   **Rank:** 1
*   **Why:** Recently transitioned 88,000+ assets to **CC0 (Public Domain)**. Provides the absolute highest standard of photographic and artistic quality available in the open-access ecosystem.
*   **Asset Types:** High-res photography, European landscape paintings, ancient manuscripts, and historical photography of global expeditions.
*   **Flagship Places Enabled:** 
    *   **Yosemite:** Early Carleton Watkins plates (Mammmoth Plates).
    *   **Machu Picchu:** Historical photography of Andean archaeology.
    *   **Galápagos:** 19th-century maritime art.

### II. Kyoto University Digital Archive
*   **Rank:** 2
*   **Why:** Provides the **Local Authority** required for the Kyoto flagship. Their transition to IIIF and open-reuse policies makes them the primary source for authentic Japanese heritage that complements the Rijksmuseum collection.
*   **Asset Types:** National Treasures, Edo-period maps, rare Japanese botanical manuscripts, and Ukiyo-e woodblock prints not found in Western collections.
*   **Flagship Places Enabled:** 
    *   **Kyoto:** Definitive maps, temple architectural drawings, and authentic Zen garden diagrams.

### III. National Library of Australia (Trove)
*   **Rank:** 3
*   **Why:** The **Primary Anchor** for the Great Barrier Reef flagship. As an aggregator, Trove provides access to Australia’s most significant scientific and historical marine records.
*   **Asset Types:** 19th-century Admiralty charts of the Coral Sea, historical reef photography (pre-1955 Public Domain), and foundational biodiversity reports.
*   **Flagship Places Enabled:** 
    *   **Great Barrier Reef:** Original Saville-Kent photography, Indigenous maritime perspectives, and historical coral-health benchmarks.
