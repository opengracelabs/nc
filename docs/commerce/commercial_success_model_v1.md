# Nature & Culture Commercial Success Model (CSM) v1.0

The Commercial Success Model (CSM) is the strategic framework used by Nature & Culture to predict the commercial viability of heritage assets. It synthesizes principles from museum retail, archive commerce, tourism, and premium publishing into a quantitative scoring system.

---

## 1. Scoring Dimensions

Every Illustration Opportunity is evaluated against seven core dimensions to calculate its **Commercial Success Probability (CSP)**.

### 1.1 Visual Authority Score (VAS) - 30%
*   **Definition:** The intrinsic aesthetic power of the asset.
*   **Success Predictors:** Hand-colored lithography, "Golden Age" (1750–1900) refinement, compositional balance, and clarity of subject.
*   **Commerce Impact:** High VAS is the #1 predictor of "Impulse Buy" conversions for wall art and decor.

### 1.2 Place Identity Score (PIS) - 20%
*   **Definition:** The strength of the asset's connection to a specific geography (e.g., Flagship 70).
*   **Success Predictors:** Direct naming in titles/metadata, "Sense of Place" visuals (maps, landmarks).
*   **Commerce Impact:** Drives search discoverability. Buyers search for *places* before they search for *artists*.

### 1.3 Story Strength Score (SSS) - 15%
*   **Definition:** The depth of the narrative or provenance surrounding the asset.
*   **Success Predictors:** Linkage to famous expeditions (e.g., HMS Beagle, Hayden Survey), master illustrators (e.g., Audubon), or "First Instance" discoveries.
*   **Commerce Impact:** The "Premium Multiplier." Strong stories justify higher price points for books and archival prints.

### 1.4 Tourism Appeal Score (TAS) - 15%
*   **Definition:** The asset's ability to trigger "Travel Nostalgia" or bucket-list desire.
*   **Success Predictors:** Iconic status of the site, high tourism volume (e.g., Yellowstone vs. a remote MPA).
*   **Commerce Impact:** Directly correlates with sales of "Keepsake" products (Calendars, Postcards, Apparel).

### 1.5 Institutional Prestige Score (IPS) - 10%
*   **Definition:** The authority of the source institution.
*   **Success Predictors:** Library of Congress (LOC), Smithsonian, British Museum, etc.
*   **Commerce Impact:** Acts as a trust anchor. Institutional branding increases "Giftability" and collector confidence.

### 1.6 Product Versatility Score (PVS) - 10%
*   **Definition:** The technical and aesthetic adaptability of the asset across different product types.
*   **Success Predictors:** High-resolution TIF availability (600 DPI+), tile-ability for patterns, narrative density for publishing.
*   **Commerce Impact:** Maximizes the Lifetime Value (LTV) of a single ingestion across multiple product lines (Wall Art → Puzzles → Books).

### 1.7 Rights Clarity Score (RCS) - Hard Gate
*   **Definition:** The verification of Public Domain or CC0 status.
*   **Requirement:** Must be 100/100 (Verified) for ingestion.
*   **Commerce Impact:** Zero-risk scaling. Blocked assets never enter the commerce pipeline.

## 2. Classification Framework

The aggregate **Commercial Success Probability (CSP)** determines the asset's path.

| Classification | CSP Score | Strategic Action | Channel Deployment |
| :--- | :---: | :--- | :--- |
| **MASTERWORK** | 90–100 | **Premium Anchor.** Invest in high-res restoration. | Archival Prints, Limited Folios, Coffee Table Books. |
| **FLAGSHIP** | 75–89 | **Core Revenue.** Primary focus for product generation. | Wall Art, Calendars, Puzzles, Apparel. |
| **STANDARD** | 60–74 | **Long-Tail Volume.** Automated deployment. | Etsy/Amazon listings, Postcards, Stationery. |
| **REFERENCE** | 40–59 | **Education Only.** Do not deploy to commerce. | Knowledge Profile, Field Guides, Research Folios. |
| **BLOCKED** | < 40 | **Discard.** Low quality or high risk. | None. |

## 3. Yellowstone Heritage Examples

Applying the CSM v1.0 to foundational Yellowstone assets.

### 3.1 1871 Hayden Survey Map (LOC GMD)
*   **Classification:** **MASTERWORK**
*   **CSP Score:** **96**
*   **Analysis:** 
    *   **Visual Authority:** 10/10 (High-detail historical cartography).
    *   **Place Identity:** 10/10 (The foundational map of the park).
    *   **Story Strength:** 10/10 (The "Blueprint of Wilderness" that convinced Congress).
    *   **Commercial Path:** Premium full-scale archival linen prints and the anchor for the "Expedition 1871" book.

### 3.2 Thomas Moran, *The Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone* (1872)
*   **Classification:** **MASTERWORK**
*   **CSP Score:** **98**
*   **Analysis:** 
    *   **Visual Authority:** 10/10 (Highest tier of American landscape art).
    *   **Institutional Prestige:** 10/10 (Smithsonian/National Gallery status).
    *   **Tourism Appeal:** 10/10 (The iconic "Hero Image" of the park).
    *   **Commercial Path:** High-end fine art prints and luxury home decor.

### 3.3 William Henry Jackson, *Old Faithful* (1872 Photography)
*   **Classification:** **FLAGSHIP**
*   **CSP Score:** **84**
*   **Analysis:** 
    *   **Visual Authority:** 8/10 (Authentic, high-contrast B&W photography).
    *   **Story Strength:** 9/10 (First photographic evidence of geothermal wonders).
    *   **Product Versatility:** 10/10 (Works across posters, calendars, and heritage apparel).
    *   **Commercial Path:** Heritage series calendars and "Surveyor" style t-shirts/posters.

## 4. Summary Recommendation

To ensure v1 profitability, the **Chief Curator** should prioritize **Masterwork** assets with **CSP > 90**. These assets act as the "Marketing Halo" for the entire platform, establishing the brand's commitment to Museum-Grade quality while providing the highest individual unit margins.
