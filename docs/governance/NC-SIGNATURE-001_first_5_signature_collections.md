### Ranking rationale for ties

**Alhambra ahead of Petra (#1 vs. #2):** Both score identically across all four criteria.

via the Roberts itinerary, and independently to Samarkand and Isfahan via the Islamic sphere.

The more central node carries the higher rank.

**South Georgia at #3:** Scores identically to Alhambra and Petra. It is ranked third not
because it is weaker — it is not — but because it anchors a different journey type
(Expedition Trail) than Alhambra and Petra (Illustrator's World + Cultural Sphere). Both
journey types are needed. The Expedition Trail has the widest geographic reach (44 places)
but lower commerce conversion than the Islamic sphere chain. Third is not a demotion.

**Hudson River ahead of Kyoto (#4 vs. #5):** Both score 19/19. Hudson River's discovery
graph connects to four *existing* NC pilot places (Yellowstone, Yosemite, Grand Canyon,
Olympic) on day one. Kyoto's discovery connections (Samarkand, Isfahan, Angkor, Borobudur)
are in the activation queue but not yet live. At launch, immediate graph connectivity
outweighs future connectivity. Kyoto closes the set with the highest composite activation
score (91) and anchors the ICH Thread, which is otherwise absent from the top 5.

### Rank 1 — NC-COL-001: The Alhambra

*"The Grammar of Ornament"*

**Place:** `alhambra-generalife` · Spain · C.HU.RY
**Institution:** Europeana · Walters
**Score:** 20/20 · Hub rating: ★★★★★ (highest of all 10)

#### Why #1

Owen Jones did not go to the Alhambra to admire it. He went to decode it. The 102
chromolithograph plates he produced in *Plans and Details of the Alhambra* (1842–45)
are the most forensically precise architectural illustration of the Islamic Golden Age —
every tile grid measured, every muqarnas section drawn to scale. When he returned to those
plates for *The Grammar of Ornament* (1856), he extracted from Moorish design a universal
system of pattern that shaped the Great Exhibition, the V&A's founding methodology, and a
century of industrial textile production. The plates have been in continuous commercial use
for 170 years. No other PD illustration in the NC portfolio has that record.

As a discovery hub, the Alhambra is the central node of the Islamic sphere: one traversal
reaches Petra (via Roberts's itinerary), Fez (same itinerary), and Samarkand and Isfahan
(via the Islamic Cultural Sphere axis). It is the only collection that simultaneously anchors
the Illustrator's World journey (Roberts), the Cultural Sphere journey (Islamic world), and
the ICH Thread (Moorish geometric → Arabic calligraphy → Persian carpet).

#### Signature Image

Owen Jones, *Plate VI: Section through the Hall of the Abencerrages, Alhambra* (1842) —
muqarnas vaulting rendered in full chromolithographic colour. Available: Europeana.
Rights: Public domain. Resolution: confirmed high-res.

#### The Story

An architect arrives in Granada in 1834 with a portable lithography press. He stays for
eighteen months. He draws every surface. When he leaves, he publishes the most complete
visual record ever made of Nasrid geometry — and then uses it to argue, in a second book
twenty years later, that ornament has grammatical rules. The Great Exhibition of 1851 is
designed according to those rules. The V&A is founded in the same year with those rules
as its curatorial foundation. Every wallpaper, textile, and tile pattern produced by the
Victorian decorative arts industry takes its proportional system from Owen Jones's eighteen
months in Granada.

### Rank 2 — NC-COL-005: Petra

*"The Rose-Red City"*

**Place:** `petra` · Jordan · C.HR.TR
**Institution:** Walters · Europeana
**Score:** 20/20 · Hub rating: ★★★★★

#### Why #2

David Roberts produced 247 hand-coloured lithographs across six volumes of *The Holy Land,
Syria, Idumea, Arabia* (1842–49). This is the deepest single-illustrator, single-journey PD
set available to NC — and the plates he made of Petra are its commercial centrepiece.
The *Khazneh* (Treasury) plate — rose-red Nabataean façade framed by the siq canyon —
is the single most commercially recognisable Near East illustration in the 19th-century
travel record. It was subscribed before publication by Queen Victoria, the Tsar of Russia,
and the Sultan of the Ottoman Empire. It has been in continuous commercial reproduction
for 183 years.

Petra scores identically to the Alhambra on all four criteria. It ranks second because its
graph reaches fewer nodes in a single traversal: Petra connects outward to Alhambra and Fez
(via Roberts), but those connections run inward toward the hub (the Alhambra) rather than
outward from it. Alhambra is the source; Petra is a spoke.

#### Signature Image

David Roberts, *The Treasury of Petra (Khazneh)* — hand-coloured lithograph from *The Holy
Land* Vol. III (1843). Available: Europeana. Rights: Public domain. Resolution: confirmed.
The most reproduced 19th-c. Near East travel illustration in the NC portfolio.

#### The Story

The city was built by frankincense traders and abandoned when the trade routes moved.
For 1,600 years it was known only to Bedouin. In 1812, a Swiss explorer named Johann Ludwig
Burckhardt disguised himself as a Muslim pilgrim to enter the valley, became the first
European in the modern era to see the Khazneh, and told no one for years. David Roberts
arrived 27 years later without disguise, set up his perspective apparatus in the siq, and
spent two weeks drawing. The lithographs he made from those drawings were on sale across
Europe within five years. Since then, the only image most people have of Petra is Roberts's.

### Rank 3 — NC-COL-002: South Georgia

*"The Far Shore"*

**Place:** `south-georgia-island` · UK (South Atlantic) · N.IS.SB
**Institution:** NHM (primary)
**Score:** 20/20 · Hub rating: ★★★★★ (widest expedition chain of all 10)

#### Why #3

Three separate expeditions found this island indispensable. Cook arrived in 1775, declared
the place worthless, and left the most detailed first-encounter natural history record of
any sub-Antarctic island. Banks's botanical notes from that same landing proved the island
sits at a three-current confluence that makes it the most biologically productive shore in
the Southern Ocean. A century later, Edward Wilson — Scott's chief of scientific staff and
the finest Antarctic painter of his generation — spent six weeks at South Georgia's king
penguin colonies making watercolour studies that remain the definitive natural history
illustration of the species. The NHM holds the originals. They are CC0.

As an expedition hub, South Georgia is without peer in this portfolio. Two full expedition
families radiate from it: the Cook voyage family (24 places platform-wide) and the Challenger
expedition family (20 places). No other collection activates 44 discovery pathways in a
single traversal. This is not a spoke. It is a second hub, of a different kind.

#### Signature Image

Edward Adrian Wilson, *King Penguin and Chick, South Georgia* (c. 1903–04) — watercolour
on paper. NHM. CC0. Wilson was the only Antarctic painter working at this level. The image
is both scientifically exact and aesthetically irreplaceable — the two qualities that define
the NC commercial object.

#### The Story

The island appears three times in British history and each time the stakes are higher. Cook
comes in 1775 and finds penguins. Wilson comes in 1901 and paints them — not as subjects,
but as individuals, each bird a distinct study in posture and relationship. Shackleton comes
in 1916 after crossing 800 miles of open ocean in a 22-foot boat following the wreck of
*Endurance*, and drags himself to the Stromness whaling station knowing that every person
he left behind on Elephant Island is still alive because he made it. The penguins were
there for all of it. Wilson painted them. The NHM holds the paintings.

### Rank 4 — NC-COL-007: Hudson River

*"The School of the Wild"*

**Place:** `hudson-river-highlands` · USA · C.CL.EV
**Institution:** NGA (primary, CC0 confirmed)
**Score:** 19/20 · Hub rating: ★★★★☆

#### Why #4

The NGA is the cleanest rights position in the entire NC portfolio. Every work in its open
access collection is CC0 — no per-image licensing, no institutional approval process, no
resolution limits. At launch, this removes an entire category of operational risk that the
other four signature collections carry. That matters.

But the rights advantage is secondary to the discovery argument. Hudson River ranks fourth
because it connects to four existing NC pilot places on day one — Yellowstone, Yosemite,
Grand Canyon, and Olympic are all live in the NC portfolio, and all four carry the chain of
influence that runs from Thomas Cole through Frederic Church to Thomas Moran, who painted
all of them. At launch, when the NC discovery graph is thin, every live connection counts
more than a projected one. Hudson River turns four existing pilot places from endpoints
into waypoints on a continuous American landscape journey.

Kyoto scores equally but its connections run forward, to places not yet live. Hudson River
connects backward, to a discovery graph that is open today.

#### Signature Image

Thomas Cole, *The Voyage of Life: Youth* (1842) — oil on canvas. NGA. CC0. Cole's
four-part allegorical sequence depicting the passage of human life through a river landscape
is the most ambitious single artistic project in the Hudson River School canon — and the
most emotionally available. Every viewer recognises the young man in the boat.

#### The Story

Cole arrived from Lancashire in 1818 and walked up the Hudson Valley in the autumn. He
knew immediately that the forest was going to be cut. He painted it not as it was but as
it deserved to be remembered — vast, still, lit from inside. The paintings he produced in
the 1830s and 1840s launched a cultural movement that Emerson articulated, Thoreau wrote
about, and Muir operationalised. Yellowstone was designated a national park in 1872.
Cole had been dead for twenty-four years. His paintings had been in circulation for thirty.
The first national park in the world was, in part, a response to the argument Cole made
in oil on canvas before the language of conservation existed.

### Rank 5 — NC-COL-006: Kyoto

*"The Thousand-Year Capital"*

**Place:** `kyoto` · Japan · C.HU.RY
**Institution:** Met (primary) · CMA (primary)
**Score:** 19/20 · Hub rating: ★★★★★

#### Why #5

Kyoto carries the highest composite activation score (91) of any place in the NC portfolio.
It is ranked fifth — not first — because its most commercially valuable products are
SA-blocked. The ICH TC path (Nishijin-ori silk textile design) requires SA-ICH-PLACE-001
before activation, and SA-ICH-PLACE-001 is the same standard that unlocks the calligraphy
products at Fez and the ikat products at Samarkand. Until that SA is ratified, Kyoto's
launch products — Buddhist temple prints, court painting reproductions, lacquerware
illustration — are excellent but lack the single dominant commerce anchor that the other
four signature collections have in their first product.

It ranks fifth rather than not-at-all for three reasons. First, it anchors the ICH Thread
journey — the most commercially valuable discovery chain on the platform — and that journey
is otherwise absent from the top 5 without it. Second, it represents East Asia; without
Kyoto, the five signature collections cover three European/Near Eastern traditions and one
American one. Third, once SA-ICH-PLACE-001 is ratified, Kyoto moves to the top of the
commerce stack. The position is temporary. The anchor is permanent.

#### Signature Image

CMA, *Scenes in and around Kyoto (Rakuchū-rakugai-zu)* — folding screen, gold leaf on
paper, Edo period (17th–18th c.). CMA. CC0. The *Rakuchū-rakugai-zu* genre was the primary
visual record of Kyoto's urban life across six hundred years — the same street, the same
festivals, the same shrine, repainted every generation. The CMA holds three examples.
No other collection presents a place being repainted by the same tradition over 600 years.

#### The Story

Kyoto was the imperial capital for 1,074 years. Every shogunate that displaced the Emperor
left the city standing — it was simultaneously too sacred to destroy and too useful to
abandon. The result is an archive without parallel: 17 UNESCO World Heritage Sites, five
ICH elements, the complete sequence of Japanese aesthetic development from Heian court
painting through Zen rock garden to Edo lacquerwork, all within a single city. In 1944,
Secretary of War Henry Stimson removed Kyoto from the American bombing list. He had
honeymooned there in 1926. He understood what it was. The Nishijin silk weavers who
supplied the imperial court in the 8th century are still threading the same patterns in the
same quarter. The festival floats in the Gion Matsuri are maintained by the same 32 families
they have always been maintained by. Continuity at this scale is the rarest thing in the world.

## The 5 as a Set

### What they are together

The five signature collections are not five separate products. They are five entry points
into a single, traversable heritage archive.

```
ALHAMBRA ──── Roberts trail ──── PETRA
    │                               │
    │ Islamic sphere (AX-HIS)       │ Near East chain
    │                               │
    └──── FEZ (COL-010, Phase 2) ───┘

SOUTH GEORGIA ──── Cook voyage (24 places) ──── CANARY ISLANDS (COL-008, Phase 2)
                                                      │
                                              Humboldt chain (12 places)

HUDSON RIVER ──── American landscape chain ──── Yellowstone · Yosemite · Grand Canyon (pilot)

KYOTO ──── ICH Thread ──── Fez · Samarkand · Isfahan (Phase 2 unlocks)
    │
    └──── Buddhist sphere ──── ANGKOR (COL-009, Phase 2)
```

A visitor who starts at the Alhambra and follows the Roberts trail will arrive at Petra
in two clicks. From Petra they can follow the Near East chain forward or the Islamic sphere
back. From South Georgia they can follow Cook to Tonga, the Canary Islands, and Tierra del
Fuego. From Hudson River they arrive at Yellowstone and the American West, which leads to
Yosemite and Olympic. From Kyoto they reach Angkor by Buddhist sphere or Fez by craft chain.

No collection in the top 5 is a dead end. Every one opens outward.
