## Governing Principle

This document answers a single operational question: **of the 100 Masterpieces registered in NC-MASTERPIECES-001, which 25 activate first, in what order, and what must happen before each one goes live?**

A Masterpiece that cannot be deployed is an incomplete institutional declaration. NC-MASTERPIECES-002 converts the registry into a sequenced activation programme. Every rank here reflects deployability as much as quality — the highest-scoring works that remain blocked are ranked accordingly, with a visible accounting of what breaks the block.

The Masterpiece designation carries five standing obligations from NC-MASTERPIECES-001. Those obligations do not change with activation order. What changes is when NC assumes them.

## 1. Ranking Criteria

NC-MASTERPIECES-002 uses the NC-MASTERPIECES-001 six-criterion framework with one substitution: **Discovery Graph Value (DG)** — which measures internal graph connectivity — is replaced by **Collection Value (CV)** — which measures editorial weight, provenance uniqueness, and irreplaceability within NC's curated collection. This substitution is appropriate for an activation context where curatorial significance governs commercial sequencing.

The six criteria, in the priority order specified for this portfolio:

| # | Code | Criterion | What it measures |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **CS** | Cultural Significance | Recognition beyond specialist circles. Does this work appear in histories of art, science, or exploration? Is it reproducible in NC's brand story? |
| 2 | **SS** | Scientific Significance | Did this illustration advance or record scientific understanding? Does it document species, phenomena, or discoveries of lasting importance? |
| 3 | **PD** | Public-Domain Confidence | Rights clearance certainty. Equivalent to RC in NC-MASTERPIECES-001. Reflects current DD ratification status. |
| 4 | **CV** | Collection Value | Uniqueness within NC's curated collection. Considers: is this the only known quality illustration of this subject? Is the subject extinct or lost? Is this the signature work of a Priority Illustrator? Does it represent a first expedition depiction? |
| 5 | **CoV** | Commerce Value | Family A (Museum Giclée) viability. CE allocation eligibility. Multi-product-family reach. Equivalent to PV in NC-MASTERPIECES-001. |
| 6 | **EV** | Educational Value | Curriculum anchor potential. Cross-disciplinary appeal across biology, art history, geography, conservation, and ecology. |

**Maximum score: 60.** Activation candidate threshold for this portfolio: ≥ 53/60.

## 4. Editorial Annotations: Top 10

### Rank 1 — Thylacine (Tasmanian Tiger) · Gould · NHM · 59/60

The highest-scoring work in the portfolio. No comparable natural history illustration exists: a now-confirmed extinct apex predator, painted at peak scientific precision by NC's second Priority Illustrator in the year before the species' colonial extirpation accelerated. The NHM holds Gould's original watercolours under CC0. Commercial and conservation narratives converge completely — every product is simultaneously a study in Victorian natural history mastery and an extinction record. The Thylacine is NC's single most important acquisition from DD-NHM-001.

**Place connection:** Tasmania / Tasmanian Wilderness World Heritage Area.  
**Blocking action:** DD-NHM-001 ratification.

---

### Rank 2–3 — Carolina Parakeet + Passenger Pigeon · Audubon · BHL / Smithsonian

The Extinction Archive's defining pair. Both species are gone. Both were among the most abundant birds in North America. Audubon painted both at the height of his powers. Together they constitute the single most powerful conservation narrative in NC's entire portfolio — and the most straightforward editorial argument for the Extinction Archive product series. BHL versions are Wave 1. Smithsonian high-resolution upgrade is the highest-priority output of DD-SMITHSONIAN-001.

**Note:** These two works should be activated together and sold as a paired portfolio. Neither is optimised for solo deployment.  
**Place connections:** Appalachian forests / Eastern North America / Great Lakes region.

---

### Rank 4 — Radiolaria (Pl. 1) · Haeckel · BHL T1 · 58/60

The most recognisable scientific illustration in the Western world after Darwin's Finches. Haeckel's Radiolaria plate is the origin point of Art Nouveau's organic geometry — directly influencing Guimard's Paris Métro, Sullivan's ornament, and a century of decorative arts. It is also rigorous science: the first systematic classification of single-celled marine organisms. The convergence of artistic influence and scientific authority in a single plate, freely available from multiple BHL T1 sources at print resolution, makes this NC's single most commercially versatile Wave 1 deployment. No blocking actions.

**Place connection:** Open Ocean — global; anchors NC's future oceanographic collection tier.

---

### Rank 5 — Darwin's Finches · Gould / Darwin · BHL · 58/60 · ACTIVE

The illustration of evolution. John Gould redrew Darwin's field sketches from the Beagle voyage and classified four finch species — the visual evidence that Darwin himself used to reconstruct the theory of natural selection. NC-PROD-005 is the first commercial activation of this work. No other Illustration Opportunity in NC's portfolio carries equivalent scientific weight. The Galápagos place connection is active; the discovery graph connects directly to Humboldt and the broader Andes expedition chain.

**Note:** Already in production as NC-PROD-005. Rank 5 reflects Galápagos SA ratification status (in progress); full commercial clearance pending.

---

### Rank 6 — Platypus · Nodder · NHM CC0 · 58/60

Frederick Nodder's 1799 plate of the duck-billed platypus is one of the most consequential moments in natural history illustration. When the platypus specimen arrived in Britain, the scientific establishment initially believed it a hoax — a duck's bill sewn to a beaver. Nodder's careful, accurate illustration from Banks' collection was the primary documentary evidence confirming the animal's existence. There is no substitute. The NHM holds the original and has confirmed CC0 status. DD-NHM-001 is the sole blocking action for what is otherwise a definitive, commercially deployable Wave 2 masterpiece.

**Place connection:** Botany Bay / Eastern Australia — opens Australia content tier.

---

### Rank 7 — Marvelous Spatuletail · Gould · BHL / NHM T1 · 58/60

Loddigesia mirabilis: a critically endangered hummingbird endemic to a single valley in northern Peru, with a tail twice the length of its body and iridescent violet disc-tipped rackets. Gould illustrated this species at the height of his monograph programme — it is simultaneously the most visually spectacular plate in the Trochilidae series and one of the rarest subjects in the hummingbird trade today. The BHL version is Wave 1. The NHM high-resolution version follows DD-NHM-001. Commerce value is 10/10: no other NC illustration combines extreme visual impact, extreme rarity, and a living endangered species narrative.

**Place connection:** Utcubamba Valley, Amazonas region, Peru — Andes-Amazon transition zone.

---

### Rank 8 — Wild Turkey · Audubon · BHL / Smithsonian · 57/60

Plate I of The Birds of America. Audubon chose the Wild Turkey as his opening declaration: the most American bird, painted life-size on double elephant folio, at a scale and ambition no natural history publication had attempted. The turkey was simultaneously Benjamin Franklin's preferred national symbol and the bird most associated with American abundance and its loss. The BHL version is Wave 1 activatable. The Smithsonian elephant folio version — at the resolution required for a true museum giclée — awaits DD-SMITHSONIAN-001. Until then, NC can activate this work; it cannot activate it at its full potential.

---

### Rank 9–10 — Bald Eagle + American Bison · Audubon · BHL · 57/60

The two national symbols of the United States, both painted by Audubon at the moment of their near-extinction and subsequent recovery. The Bald Eagle (Pl. XXXI) is activatable from BHL now. The American Bison (Quadrupeds, Pl. LVII) connects directly to Yellowstone, NC's active pilot place — the only masterpiece in this portfolio with a confirmed live place-page connection for Wave 1 deployment. Both works should enter the Yellowstone collection as NC-COL-YEL anchor assets once NARA Sprint 1 and NC-DATA-001 consolidation complete.

NC-MASTERPIECES-002 is an operational derivative of NC-MASTERPIECES-001. It does not alter any scores, series classifications, or NC-IDs in the parent registry. The ranking methodology substitutes CV for DG (see Section 1). Any work ranked in NC-MASTERPIECES-002 that receives a ratified institutional DD automatically advances to its Wave assignment. Future sprints will extend this portfolio to a Top 50 Activation Portfolio as additional DDs ratify.

Works ranked 26–100 in NC-MASTERPIECES-001 are not deactivated by this document. They are not sequenced here. They enter activation through the standard NC-IO-001 pipeline as their respective institutional DDs ratify and place pages go live.
