## Governing Principles

Four criteria scored for each collection. The commercial identity of each collection must
be a specific PD illustration вҖ” not a place, not a taxon, not a cultural practice.
The story and discovery journey amplify the illustration; they do not substitute for it.

| Criterion | What it requires |
|---|---|
| **PD assets** | A named illustrator, named expedition, or named collection with confirmed active NC institution. Specific images identified, not general depth. |
| **Story** | A narrative arc with a beginning (discovery or commission), a tension (the expedition, the court, the rediscovery), and a consequence (the image the world received). |
| **Commerce** | At minimum 3 launch-ready products. Commerce identity must be clear before Gate E. |
| **Discovery journey** | The collection activates at least 2 of the 7 canonical NC traversal axes (Expedition Trail, Illustrator's World, Designation Network, Ecological Corridor, ICH Thread, Taxon Trail, Cultural Sphere) and connects to вүҘ3 other places in the active portfolio. |

**All 10 selected collections are in NC-ACTIVATION-001 Wave 1.** Core illustration commerce
is unblocked for all 10 without requiring SA ratification. Where SA ratification unlocks
additional product lines (ICH TC), that is flagged as a Phase 2 expansion.

**The 10 are not ranked within this document.** They are ordered by their primary discovery
journey type to make the constellation logic clear.

### NC-COL-001 вҖ” The Alhambra
*"The Grammar of Ornament"*

**Place:** `alhambra-generalife` В· Spain В· C.HU.RY В· Score 88
**Institution:** Europeana (primary) В· Walters (Islamic manuscript context)

#### Anchor Illustration

Owen Jones, *Plans and Details of the Alhambra* (1842вҖ“45) вҖ” 102 hand-drawn
chromolithograph plates documenting the geometry, colour, and proportion of every surface of the
Nasrid palace. The most rigorous architectural illustration of the Islamic Golden Age.
Jones returned to the Alhambra plates for *The Grammar of Ornament* (1856), the founding
document of pattern design вҖ” 37 of its 112 plates directly quote Alhambra ornament. Both
works are PD. Europeana holds confirmed digital access.

#### The Story

In the winter of 1834, a 27-year-old Welsh architect named Owen Jones climbed to the top of the
Alhambra with a transit and a portable lithography press. For eighteen months he drew every
panel, every muqarnas, every interlaced arch вҖ” not as a ruin to be mourned, but as a
mathematical system to be understood. The plates he produced became the most influential pattern
design source of the 19th century, directly shaping the Great Exhibition (1851), the V&A's
founding collection, and the first generation of industrially printed textiles. The geometry
encoded in the red tile is still in production today.

### NC-COL-002 вҖ” South Georgia
*"The Far Shore"*

**Place:** `south-georgia-island` В· UK (South Atlantic) В· N.IS.SB В· Score 87
**Institution:** NHM (primary) вҖ” Wilson watercolours + Cook voyage + Challenger records
**Activation wave:** Wave 1 вҖ” unblocked

#### Anchor Illustration

Edward Adrian Wilson, *King Penguin and Chick* (c. 1903вҖ“04) вҖ” watercolour on paper.
Wilson was the Chief of Scientific Staff on Scott's *Discovery* and *Terra Nova* expeditions.
He painted South Georgia's colonies during the *Discovery* expedition's outward leg (1901).
The king penguin studies are among the most technically accomplished ornithological watercolours
ever made вҖ” anatomically precise, behaviourally observed, tonally subtle. NHM holds the
originals and their digitisation is confirmed CC0.

#### The Story

Three expeditions converge on the same 170-kilometre island across 130 years of British history.
Cook arrived in 1775 вҖ” the first human to set foot here, naming it for a king he was about to
lose вҖ” and declared the land worthless. Joseph Banks disagreed: his botanical records from the
brief landing proved the island sits at the intersection of three ocean currents and two
biogeographic zones. A century later, Wilson painted 300,000 king penguins from a cliff above
Gold Harbour. And in 1916, Shackleton staggered ashore at King Haakon Bay after an impossible
800-mile crossing in an open boat, knowing that a whaling station at Stromness meant his men
would live. The island has been at the centre of every significant British engagement with the
Southern Ocean. The penguins were there for all of it.

Chiapas, two on YucatГЎn вҖ” caused an international sensation. They proved that the Americas had
produced monumental architecture as complex as anything in Egypt or Greece, and they proved it
with the most technically accomplished archaeological illustration of the century.

### NC-COL-005 вҖ” Petra
*"The Rose-Red City"*

**Place:** `petra` В· Jordan В· C.HR.TR В· Score 86
**Institution:** Walters (primary) В· Europeana (primary)

#### Anchor Illustration

David Roberts, *The Treasury of Petra* (*Khazneh*) and *The Street of Facades* вҖ” lithographs
from *The Holy Land, Syria, Idumea, Arabia* (1842вҖ“49), six volumes comprising 247 hand-coloured

rose-red Nabataean Treasury framed by a narrow siq canyon вҖ” is the single most reproduced

#### The Story

The city was built by traders, not kings. The Nabataeans controlled the incense routes between
Arabia, Egypt, and Rome for four centuries, and they built their capital from the canyon walls
themselves вҖ” carved directly into Jordanian rose sandstone without mortar or foundation.
When the trade routes shifted in the 2nd century AD, the city emptied and was forgotten by
the outside world for 1,600 years. Burckhardt found it. Roberts painted it. The lithographs
Roberts made were subscribed by Queen Victoria, the Tsar, and the Sultan of the Ottoman Empire
before publication. There has never been a time since 1842 when those plates were not in print.

Cole had shown Americans what they stood to lose. The collection held at the NGA is available

### NC-COL-008 вҖ” Canary Islands
*"Humboldt's Departure"*

**Place:** `canary-islands-teide` В· Spain (Atlantic) В· N.GE.VL В· Score 83
**Institution:** NHM (primary) В· Europeana (primary)
**Activation wave:** Wave 1 (WH-N + botanical) В· Phase 2 (Geopark-specific content, after SA-GEOPARK-001)

#### Anchor Illustration

Alexander von Humboldt, *GГ©ographie des plantes dans les pays Г©quinoxiaux: Tableau physique
des Andes et Pays voisins* (1807) вҖ” the elevation profile of Teide and the Andes superimposed,
annotated with elevation-specific plant communities, atmospheric pressure, and temperature
gradients. This is the founding illustration of ecology: the first time the distribution of
living things was represented as a system across altitude and latitude rather than a list of
species. It was drawn at Teide. Joseph Banks's 1768 Canarian botanical records from the
*Endeavour* voyage provide the secondary anchor вҖ” Banks made the first systematic botanical

#### The Story

Humboldt was twenty-nine and rich. He had already applied to join three different major
expeditions and been turned away each time. In 1799 he finally chartered his own ship.
His first stop was Tenerife: he climbed Teide вҖ” then the highest peak any European had
scientifically measured вҖ” recorded temperature, atmospheric pressure, geological formation,
and vegetation at every 100-metre interval, and understood for the first time that altitude and
latitude produce the same ecological effects. He wrote twelve letters from Tenerife in five
days. The *Tableau physique* diagram he produced from those observations became the most
influential single scientific illustration in the history of natural history. Virtually every
major ecological concept of the 19th century derived from the pattern he first saw on Teide.

seeing his drawings published. The expedition illustrated his journal from his field sketches
and published it in English and French in 1863вҖ“64. Within five years, the French had declared
Cambodia a protectorate specifically to secure access to the ruins.
