## 1. The Burden of Proof: Why Provenance Matters
In a digital landscape filled with "orphaned" media and AI-generated approximations, Nature & Culture provides the **Baseline of Truth**. We believe that the value of a public-domain asset is not just in its beauty, but in its **verifiability**.

This guide explains our rigorous process for verifying the rights and history of every asset on our platform, ensuring that collectors, teachers, researchers, and journalists can use our content with absolute confidence.

## 2. Our Verification Framework: The Three Pillars

### I. Institutional Source Authority
We do not aggregate from unverified repositories. Every asset is sourced directly from a "Tier 1" institutional partner (e.g., NASA, Rijksmuseum, Smithsonian).
*   **Verification:** We maintain a direct link (Canonical URL) to the source institution’s own record.
*   **ID Mapping:** Every asset retains its original institutional identifier (e.g., NASA ID `AS08-14-2383` or RM Object Number `SK-A-2344`).

### II. Legal Status Validation
We apply a strict "Public Domain First" policy. Assets are only activated once their legal status is confirmed through one of the following:
*   **Age of Creation:** Verification that the asset was created before 1929 (USA standard) or that the creator has been deceased for >70 years.
*   **Governmental Mandate:** Works created by US Federal Government employees (e.g., NASA, USGS) are public domain under 17 U.S.C. § 105.
*   **Open Access Declaration:** Direct "Creative Commons Zero" (CC0) or "Public Domain Mark" (PDM) assignments from the holding institution.

### III. Data Integrity & Metadata
Metadata is the "Birth Certificate" of the asset. We utilize **CIDOC CRM** and **Darwin Core** standards to ensure that the historical and scientific context is preserved.
*   **Aesthetic Integrity:** We record every restoration step. If we remove foxing or correct color, the original "raw" scan remains in our archival substrate for researcher verification.

## 3. Stakeholder Guidance: How to Use Our Data

### For Collectors
**Confidence in Ownership**
Nature & Culture ensures that every "Master Restoration" print you collect is backed by a verified public-domain status. We provide a **Digital Certificate of Provenance** that links your specific artifact back to its institutional origin and legal standing.

### For Teachers
**Integrity in the Classroom**
Our assets are "Safe for Reuse." By using Nature & Culture, you are teaching students the value of primary source evidence. Our **Discovery Journeys** provide the verified context required for curriculum-aligned learning in history, science, and art.

### For Researchers
**Baseline for Science**
Researchers can rely on our archival substrate as a "Scientific Baseline." Because we preserve original institutional IDs and high-fidelity scans, our data can be used for comparative studies (e.g., comparing a 19th-century coral lithograph to modern NOAA data).

### For Journalists
**Authority in Reporting**
Every image used from Nature & Culture comes with a ready-made **Attribution Block**. We provide the exact credit lines required by institutional partners (e.g., "Image credit: NASA / Nature & Culture Archive"), ensuring your reporting meets the highest standards of transparency.
