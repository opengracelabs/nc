## Governing Doctrine

**The commercial object is an Illustration Opportunity, not an institution.**
This portfolio does not rank institutions by prestige. It ranks them by how many Illustration Opportunities they can contribute to NC's pipeline: named illustrator + confirmed PD rights + place association + quality image.

**Priority illustrators over priority collections.**
An institution that holds 100 Haeckel plates at CC0 outranks one that holds 10,000 anonymously-attributed paintings at mixed rights.

**Geographic gaps are strategic gaps.**
Institutions ranked 66–100 score lower on volume and PD value but are included because they fill the six regional zeros identified in NC-PLACES-2000: West Africa · Horn of Africa · Central Asia · Caucasus · Korea · Indian Ocean.

---

## Scoring Model

Each institution is scored on five criteria (1–10 each, max 50):

| Criterion | Definition |
|---|---|
| **V** Asset Volume | PD illustrations available and accessible for NC's pipeline (not total collection size) |
| **P** PD Value | Rights quality (CC0 preferred over PDM over NoC-US) + priority illustrator presence + golden age density |
| **C** Collection Potential | Number of place-anchored NC collections that can be built from this source |
| **D** Discovery Potential | Metadata quality, IIIF availability, API access, graph connectivity (ULAN/GBIF/GeoNames) |
| **E** Educational Value | Iconicity, curriculum alignment potential, context quality for NC educational product lines |

**Priority illustrators** (doctrine-defined): Audubon · Gould · Merian · Redouté · Lear · Nodder · Haeckel · Wolf · (extended: Jones · Roberts · Wilson · Parkinson · Hooker · Catlin · Church · Cole · Homer)

## What Gallica's Disqualification Costs

BnF/Gallica would score 49/50 and rank second only to BHL. The loss of Gallica is the single largest gap in NC's institution portfolio.

What is specifically blocked:
- Redouté originals held by BnF (multiple key titles)
- Buffon Histoire Naturelle Générale plates
- Lamarck botanical illustration
- French colonial expedition illustration (West Africa, Indochina, Pacific)
- North African illustration (Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia)
- French WH place coverage (Mont Saint-Michel, Versailles expansion, Loire Valley)

The reinstatement path (DD-GALLICA-004) requires BnF to grant a commercial license. Until that license exists, MNHN Paris (#14), BSB (#22), and BHL's French holdings (#1) are the alternative sources for French natural history illustration.
