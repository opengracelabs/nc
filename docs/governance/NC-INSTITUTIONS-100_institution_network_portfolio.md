# NC-INSTITUTIONS-100: Institution Network Portfolio

**Subject:** Strategic Strengths of Institutional Partners
**Strategy:** Optimizing Ingestion and Curation via Partner Specialization
**Status:** DRAFT
**Date:** June 13, 2026

---

## 1. Natural History Museum, London (NHM)
*   **Collection Strengths:** The global "Type Specimen" library; unrivaled biodiversity illustrations (Bauer, Sowerby); 18th-19th C. expedition records.
*   **Place Strengths:** The British Empire's historic reach; Indo-Pacific reefs; African savannas; Arctic frontiers.
*   **Educational Strengths:** Taxonomic precision (Darwin Core); evolution theory; historical ecological baselines.
*   **Commerce Strengths:** High-fidelity botanical prints; zoological plate collections; scientific field-guide replicas.

## 2. NASA
*   **Collection Strengths:** Planetary photography; orbital sensor data; astronaut photography; mission artifacts.
*   **Place Strengths:** The ultimate "Overview" perspective; Moon, Mars, and Earth as a unified system; extreme environments (Atacama, Antarctica).
*   **Educational Strengths:** Physics of light; climate science; orbital mechanics; the history of exploration.
*   **Commerce Strengths:** Large-format atmospheric prints; satellite pattern textiles; "Overview Effect" wall art.

## 3. Rijksmuseum, Amsterdam (RM)
*   **Collection Strengths:** Dutch Golden Age masterpieces; global trade archives (VOC); "The Grammar of Ornament" motifs; early global cartography.
*   **Place Strengths:** Europe; East Indies; Japan (Deshima); the high seas.
*   **Educational Strengths:** History of global trade; evolution of artistic technique; radical open-access models.
*   **Commerce Strengths:** Masterpiece restoration prints; home decor derived from ornamental motifs; historical map puzzles.

## 4. National Gallery of Art, Washington D.C. (NGA)
*   **Collection Strengths:** The American landscape canon (Moran, Bierstadt); European masters; early American cartography.
*   **Place Strengths:** The American West; National Parks (Yellowstone, Grand Canyon); Colonial Europe.
*   **Educational Strengths:** The intersection of art and conservation; national identity through landscape.
*   **Commerce Strengths:** Premium "National Park" series; high-fidelity landscape lithographs; classical art stationery.

## 5. Smithsonian Institution (SI)
*   **Collection Strengths:** The "Nation's Attic"; encyclopedic depth in anthropology, natural history, and space; The North American Indian (Curtis).
*   **Place Strengths:** North America; Central and South American archaeology; global deep-sea records.
*   **Educational Strengths:** The "Diffusion of Knowledge" mandate; multi-disciplinary discovery; indigenous knowledge systems.
*   **Commerce Strengths:** Smithsonian-branded thematic books; educational kits; ethnographic pattern textiles.

## 6. Walters Art Museum, Baltimore
*   **Collection Strengths:** Exquisite medieval manuscripts; ancient Egyptian artifacts; Byzantine and Islamic art; rare historical bindings.
*   **Place Strengths:** The Nile Valley; The Levant; Medieval Europe; The Silk Road.
*   **Educational Strengths:** History of the written word; early Egyptology; semantic data transparency (CIDOC CRM).
*   **Commerce Strengths:** Calligraphy prints; ancient motif jewelry; illuminated manuscript reproductions.

## 7. The Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York (Met)
*   **Collection Strengths:** Global encyclopedia of 5,000 years of art; Islamic geometric design; Asian ink masterpieces (Hokusai).
*   **Place Strengths:** Truly global reach; the Silk Road; Ancient Mesopotamia; Edo Japan; Dynastic China.
*   **Educational Strengths:** Cross-cultural thematic discovery; architectural history; the evolution of global aesthetics.
*   **Commerce Strengths:** Hokusai woodblock series; geometric pattern home goods; encyclopedic art volumes.

## 8. Biodiversity Heritage Library (BHL)
*   **Collection Strengths:** The world's largest open-access repository of biodiversity literature and illustration; 500 years of scientific documentation.
*   **Place Strengths:** Every terrestrial and marine biome on the planet.
*   **Educational Strengths:** The primary evidence of life on earth; history of natural science; open-data advocacy.
*   **Commerce Strengths:** Massive-scale botanical card sets; taxon-specific plate collections; historic scientific journal art.

## 9. USGS (United States Geological Survey)
*   **Collection Strengths:** Foundational topographic surveys; the Geological Atlas of the United States; early 19th C. exploration photography.
*   **Place Strengths:** The North American continent; the deep-time record of the American West.
*   **Educational Strengths:** Plate tectonics; geomorphology; the history of cartography.
*   **Commerce Strengths:** Master restoration relief maps; topographic puzzle series; "Deep Time" strata prints.

## 10. National Archives (NARA)
*   **Collection Strengths:** The primary record of US history; patent drawings; expedition logs; WPA posters; civil rights documentation.
*   **Place Strengths:** The United States and its territories; global diplomatic reach.
*   **Educational Strengths:** The value of the primary record; history of human rights; the mechanics of democracy.
*   **Commerce Strengths:** Historic WPA "See America" series; patent blueprint art; high-fidelity maps of the 19th century.
