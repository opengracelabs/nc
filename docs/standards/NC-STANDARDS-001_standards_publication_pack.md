## 1. Why Standards Matter: The Infrastructure of Wonder

Nature & Culture is not a typical media platform. It is a "Digital Smithsonian"—a high-fidelity discovery network built on the same foundations as the world's great museums and scientific archives.

We use international standards to ensure that every asset, place, and story on our platform is:
1.  **Interoperable:** It can work with other global data systems.
2.  **Verifiable:** It can be traced back to its original institutional source.
3.  **Permanent:** It is built on technologies that will remain readable for decades.

This pack explains the specific standards we use and why they matter to our community.

## 2. Our Core Technical Standards

### I. PostGIS (Spatial Authority)
**Why it exists:** Geography is the fundamental organizing principle of reality.
*   **For Visitors:** It ensures that when you explore a "Place," you are seeing data anchored in actual physical coordinates, allowing for accurate mapping and tourism journeys.
*   **For Researchers:** It allows for precise spatial queries—such as "Show all 19th-century reef sketches within 50km of this specific coordinate."

### II. Neo4j (Relationship Intelligence)
**Why it exists:** The world is a graph, not a list.
*   **For Teachers:** It powers "Thematic Bridging," allowing students to discover the hidden connections between a 19th-century sketch and a 21st-century climate baseline.
*   **For Collectors:** It provides the "Why am I seeing this?" explanation for every recommendation, grounding discovery in evidence rather than opaque algorithms.

### III. IIIF (International Image Interoperability Framework)
**Why it exists:** High-fidelity art requires deep-zoom precision.
*   **For Journalists:** It allows for the embedding of deep-zoomable, ultra-high-resolution masterpieces directly into digital articles without losing quality.
*   **For Institutions:** It ensures that Nature & Culture can serve high-fidelity media directly from institutional archives without creating redundant, low-quality copies.

## 3. Our Core Editorial & Scientific Standards

### IV. Darwin Core (Biological Data)
**Why it exists:** To speak the universal language of natural science.
*   **For Researchers:** We map every archival illustration to its `dwc:Taxon` (scientific name) and `dwc:Occurrence` (location/date). This transforms art into scientific evidence.
*   **For Teachers:** It ensures that students are learning canonical scientific classification alongside history and art.

### V. CIDOC CRM (Cultural Heritage Intelligence)
**Why it exists:** To preserve the provenance of human creativity.
*   **For Institutions:** It provides the semantic "glue" that links an artist, an object, a place, and a historical event into a single, verifiable record.
*   **For Researchers:** It ensures that "The Rediscovery" of an artifact is documented with the same rigor as the artifact itself.

### VI. RightsStatements.org & Creative Commons
**Why it exists:** To provide absolute clarity on the "Democracy of Discovery."
*   **For Collectors:** You always know exactly what you own and how you can use it.
*   **For Journalists:** Clear, machine-readable rights markers mean you can use our assets in your reporting with zero legal ambiguity.
