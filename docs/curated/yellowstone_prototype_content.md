## 2. Yellowstone Story
*Visual Suggestion: Historic Haynes stereoscopic views (CHNDM) or Hayden Expedition photography (SIA).*

**Title (EN):**  
The Living Frontier: Yellowstone National Park

**Narrative (EN):**  
Established in 1872 as the world's first national park, Yellowstone is a vast wilderness of geothermal wonders and untamed fauna. This high plateau in the heart of the Rocky Mountains preserves a unique ecological heritage that has inspired artists and scientists for over 150 years.

From the first scientific sketches of the Hayden Expedition to the majestic landscape paintings of Thomas Moran, Yellowstone’s identity is inextricably linked to the visual records preserved in our global archives. Today, we bridge this history with discovery, inviting you to explore the "Icons of the American West" through the lens of those who first documented its grandeur.

## 3. Collection Narrative
*Collection: Yellowstone: Icons of the American West*

**Title (EN):**  
Icons of the American West: The Audubon Folio

**Narrative (EN):**  
This curated collection features the monumental lithographs from John James Audubon and John Bachman’s *The Viviparous Quadrupeds of North America* (1845–1848). Drawn from the Biodiversity Heritage Library, these plates represent the pinnacle of 19th-century natural history illustration. 

Focusing on the megafauna that still roam the Greater Yellowstone Ecosystem—the American Bison, the Grizzly Bear, and the Gray Wolf—this collection celebrates the raw power and biological diversity of the American frontier. Each plate is a testament to Audubon's final expedition to the Upper Missouri, capturing the essence of species that define the Yellowstone wilderness.

## 5. Illustrator Narrative: John James Audubon

**Title (EN):**  
John James Audubon (1785–1851)

**Narrative (EN):**  
The most celebrated wildlife artist in American history, Audubon combined scientific precision with dramatic, life-sized composition. While world-renowned for his *Birds of America*, his final great work, *The Viviparous Quadrupeds of North America*, remains the definitive visual record of North American mammals in the 19th century. His work serves as the foundational visual archive for the Nature & Culture Yellowstone collection.
