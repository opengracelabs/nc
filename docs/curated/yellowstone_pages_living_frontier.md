*   **Description:** The raw power of the Bison, Grizzly, and Wolf, captured in the hand-colored lithographs of John James Audubon.

## 3. Yellowstone Collection Page: "Icons of the American West"

### **A. Hero Content**
*   **Visual:** Detail of BHL Plate 56 (American Bison).
*   **Title:** Icons of the American West: The Audubon Folio.
*   **Source:** Biodiversity Heritage Library.

### **B. Story Content**
*   **Title:** The Final Expedition.
*   **Narrative:** John James Audubon’s final great work, *The Viviparous Quadrupeds of North America*, was born from a rugged journey up the Missouri River in 1843. These plates are more than illustrations; they are the definitive visual archive of the majestic species—Bison, Grizzly, and Wolf—that define the spirit of Yellowstone.

### **C. Discovery Content**
*   **Filters:**
    *   *Taxon:* Bison, Ursus, Canis, Cervus.
    *   *Era:* 1845–1848 (Imperial Folio).

### **D. Collection Content**
*   **Asset Grid:** The 6 core flagship plates (Bison Male/Female, Grizzly, Wolf, Elk, Bighorn Sheep) with deep-zoom capability and BHL provenance.

### **E. Commerce Content**
*   **Headline:** Acquire the Masterworks.
*   **CTA:** Shop the Audubon Folio.
