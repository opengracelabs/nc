# Yellowstone Prototype: Reviewer Script

**Objective:** Evaluate the Yellowstone prototype pages for storytelling impact, alignment with the "Living Frontier" brand archetype, and world-class user experience.

---

## 1. Place Page: Yellowstone National Park
*Focus: Primordial grandeur and the "World's First" narrative.*

### **Storytelling Audit**
- [ ] **Archetype Check:** Does the "Living Frontier" voice (authoritative and rugged) resonate?
- [ ] **Archive Bridge:** Is the connection between the 1871 Hayden Survey (Smithsonian) and 19th-century zoology (BHL) explicit and compelling?
- [ ] **Visual Impact:** Does the hero landscape (Thomas Moran) evoke the intended "Awe and Reverence"?

### **UX & Navigation Audit**
- [ ] **Mobile Map:** Is the "Expedition Records" map easy to navigate on a small screen? Does it avoid "scroll trapping"?
- [ ] **Hierarchy:** Are the "Discovery Opportunities" (Archive Grid) clearly distinguished from the "Story" narrative?
- [ ] **Language:** Toggle to German (DE). Do the designation labels (e.g., "UNESCO World Heritage Site") overflow or break?

---

## 2. Collection Page: Icons of the American West
*Focus: The Audubon legacy and scientific precision.*

### **Storytelling Audit**
- [ ] **Historical Context:** Is the story of Audubon's "Final Expedition" clearly told? Does it add value beyond just "looking at pictures"?
- [ ] **Curation Logic:** Do the selected assets (Bison, Grizzly, Wolf) feel like a coherent "Icon" set?

### **UX & Navigation Audit**
- [ ] **Filter Clarity:** Are the Taxonomy and Medium filters intuitive?
- [ ] **Grid Performance:** Does the asset grid load smoothly? Does the "Deep Zoom" affordance look inviting?
- [ ] **Accessibility:** Are the dynamic BHL titles readable? Is there enough contrast between the text and the archive thumbnails?

---

## 3. Asset Page: Individual Masterworks
*Focus: Deep-zoom discovery and provenance.*

### **Storytelling Audit**
- [ ] **Micro-Narrative:** Does the "Asset Narrative" explain *why* this specific plate is significant to Yellowstone's history?
- [ ] **Attribution:** Is the BHL/Smithsonian source attribution prominent and respectful?

### **UX & Navigation Audit**
- [ ] **Deep Zoom:** Is the zoom interaction fluid? On mobile, can you pinch-to-zoom easily without UI clutter?
- [ ] **Metadata Layer:** Is the provenance (Year, Creator, Rights) accessible but not overwhelming ("Generous Interface" principle)?
- [ ] **Multilingual Titles:** Toggle to Japanese (JA) or Chinese (ZH). Is the font choice appropriate for natural history terminology?

---

## 4. Product Page: Heritage Fine Art Prints
*Focus: Commercial integrity and "Bring the Frontier Home."*

### **Storytelling Audit**
- [ ] **Value Bridge:** Does the product description successfully link the physical object to the archival story?
- [ ] **Quality Cues:** Does the "Craft" narrative (Acid-free paper, Archival inks) maintain the "Authority" voice?

### **UX & Navigation Audit**
- [ ] **Configurator Flow:** Is selecting a size and frame straightforward?
- [ ] **Accessibility:** Can a screen reader easily navigate the variant selectors?
- [ ] **Trust Signals:** Is the rights status (CC0/Public Domain) clearly reinforced to ensure the customer knows they are buying "Heritage"?
