# ALA Discovery Portfolio v1

**Role:** Chief Curator / Biodiversity Architect  
**Objective:** Identify the top 50 high-impact biodiversity assets from the Atlas of Living Australia (ALA) to establish Nature & Culture's "Ecological Authority" for the Australian continent.

---

## 1. Portfolio Strategy: The "Living Continent"

This portfolio prioritizes assets that document the unique evolutionary history of Australia, focusing on endemism, pollination syndromes, and critical conservation sites. While ALA is primarily an occurrence database (see **DD-ALA-001** for commercial illustration disqualification), these assets serve as the **Scientific Scaffolding** for place-centered discovery.

### Ranking Criteria:
1.  **Ecological Significance:** Does the species anchor a specific Australian ecosystem?
2.  **Endemism:** Is the species found nowhere else on Earth?
3.  **Conservation Urgency:** Is the species listed as Threatened or Endangered?
4.  **Place Association:** Does the record link to a National Park or Ramsar Wetland?
5.  **Discovery Potential:** Does the asset explain a "Nature & Culture" narrative (e.g., indigenous foodways or pollination)?

---

## 2. Recommended Asset Zero: The "Ecological Anchor"

### **The Koala (Phascolarctos cinereus)**
*   **Asset:** *Phascolarctos cinereus* (Koala).
*   **Category:** Endemic / Threatened / Iconic.
*   **LSID:** `urn:lsid:biodiversity.org.au:afd.taxon:29884c7e-8519-4598-a1b7-66a9a08e0638`
*   **Rationale:** The Koala is the global symbol of Australian biodiversity. It is a specialist endemic with deep cultural ties to the Australian landscape and is currently facing severe threats from habitat loss and climate change. It anchors the "Eucalypt Forest" discovery path.
*   **Recommended Use:** The primary biodiversity anchor for the **Australian Geographic Journey**. Connects to the **Blue Mountains** and **Daintree** flagships.

## 4. Ingestion Strategy: Biodiversity Metadata Only

Per **DD-ALA-001**, ALA is not a source for commercial illustration plates. Ingestion via the ALA adapter is restricted to:
1.  **Taxon Profiles:** Names, descriptions, and conservation status.
2.  **Occurrence Heatmaps:** Spatial density data for Place Intelligence.
3.  **Scientific Metadata:** LSIDs and reconciliation keys for BHL and Wikidata.
