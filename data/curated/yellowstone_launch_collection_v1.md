# Yellowstone Launch Collection v1: The MVE (Minimum Viable Experience)

**Role:** Chief Curator  
**Objective:** Select the absolute "Top 10" assets from the Europeana collection to deliver a world-class experience of Wonder, Trust, and Connection.

---

## 1. The Core Strategy: "The Living Frontier"

To succeed with only 10 assets, we must select those that are **Multimodal**—acting simultaneously as visual anchors, narrative triggers, and commercial drivers.

---

## 2. The Launch Top 10

### 1. Henry Wood Elliott: Bathymetric Map of Yellowstone Lake (1871)
*   **Why it belongs:** The foundational spatial authority.
*   **The Story:** The moment discovery turned into science.
*   **Commerce:** High (Detailed historical map prints).
*   **Education:** Teaches the geology of high-altitude alpine lakes.
*   **Tourism:** Defines the geographic heart of the park.

### 2. Thomas Moran: Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone (Chromolithograph)
*   **Why it belongs:** The definitive "Sublime" visual icon.
*   **The Story:** The visual proof that convinced the world of Yellowstone's majesty.
*   **Commerce:** Very High (Premium wall art and gallery prints).
*   **Education:** Illustrates the role of art in the conservation movement.
*   **Tourism:** Inspires the "Must-See" pilgrimage to the Artist Point.

### 3. William Henry Jackson: Old Faithful Geyser (Stereoview, 1871)
*   **Why it belongs:** The first photographic proof of the geyser's power.
*   **The Story:** Transforming "myth" into undeniable reality.
*   **Commerce:** Medium (3D-effect cards and vintage photography prints).
*   **Education:** Demonstrates early photographic techniques and geyser mechanics.
*   **Tourism:** Anchors the "Old Faithful" iconic experience.

### 4. Carleton Watkins: Mammoth Hot Springs Terraces (Albumen Print)
*   **Why it belongs:** Unrivaled photographic fidelity and technical mastery.
*   **The Story:** The "Terraces of Fire" that greet visitors from the north.
*   **Commerce:** High (Minimalist, high-contrast gallery photography).
*   **Education:** Teaches the chemistry of hydrothermal calcium carbonate deposits.
*   **Tourism:** Highlights the unique "Living" nature of the hydrothermal crust.

### 5. Northern Pacific Railway: "Yellowstone: The Wonderland" (Poster, c. 1910)
*   **Why it belongs:** The primary commercial aesthetic of the "Wonderland" era.
*   **The Story:** The birth of national park tourism as a global phenomenon.
*   **Commerce:** Very High (Vintage posters, puzzles, and stationery).
*   **Education:** Analyzes the history of marketing and public-land use.
*   **Tourism:** Evokes the "Classic Journey" by rail and stagecoach.

### 6. WPA Yellowstone Poster (1938)
*   **Why it belongs:** Iconic mid-century design that resonates with modern audiences.
*   **The Story:** The democratization of the parks during the New Deal era.
*   **Commerce:** Very High (Screen-printed posters, apparel, and cards).
*   **Education:** Discusses the intersection of government, labor, and public art.
*   **Tourism:** Promotes the park as a "National Commons."

### 7. Naturalis: Bison Buffalo Study (19th C Fine Art)
*   **Why it belongs:** The biological soul of the park in a classic, authoritative style.
*   **The Story:** The survival and restoration of the American Bison.
*   **Commerce:** High (Botanical/Biological style prints for decor).
*   **Education:** Links fauna to the broader ecosystem and conservation history.
*   **Tourism:** Highlights the "Wildlife Safari" aspect of the park.

### 8. National Library of France: The Morning Mist, Firehole River (Photography)
*   **Why it belongs:** The "Atmospheric Hero" for the intro experience.
*   **The Story:** The quiet, primeval "spirit" of the park at dawn.
*   **Commerce:** Medium (Calming, atmospheric wall art).
*   **Education:** Evokes the sensory reality of the hydrothermal environment.
*   **Tourism:** Inspires the "Quiet Discovery" of the backcountry.

### 9. Henry Wood Elliott: General Map of the Hayden Survey Route (1871)
*   **Why it belongs:** The narrative backbone for the scrollytelling journey.
*   **The Story:** The 1871 Expedition’s actual path through the wilderness.
*   **Commerce:** Medium (Historical map reproductions).
*   **Education:** Teaches early cartography and expedition logistics.
*   **Tourism:** Provides a "Historical Layer" to modern hiking and exploration.

### 10. Smithsonian: The First Survey Party (Group Portrait, 1871)
*   **Why it belongs:** The human element that builds trust and intimacy.
*   **The Story:** The men (Hayden, Moran, Jackson) who risked everything for the vision.
*   **Commerce:** Low (Educational postcards).
*   **Education:** Humanizes the history of science and exploration.
*   **Tourism:** Connects modern "Explorers" to their historical predecessors.
