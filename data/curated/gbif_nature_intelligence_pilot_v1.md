# GBIF Nature Intelligence Pilot v1

**Subject:** Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF)
**Framework:** 5-Way Institutional Integration (GBIF + NASA + NOAA + BHL + NARA)
**Locations:** Yellowstone, Grand Canyon, Great Barrier Reef, Papahānaumokuākea, Arctic
**Date:** June 11, 2026

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The **GBIF Nature Intelligence Pilot** demonstrates how biological occurrence data (GBIF) serves as the "Ground Truth" for the Nature & Culture platform. By layering this data with satellite observations (NASA), marine and atmospheric models (NOAA), historical literature (BHL), and federal heritage records (NARA), we create a comprehensive "Place Intelligence" engine. This pilot identifies 25 high-impact use cases and 30 narrative journeys that bridge the gap between scientific data and cultural storytelling.

## 3. Top 10 Biodiversity Journeys
*Focus: Evolutionary depth and species richness.*

1.  **The Great Migration (Caribou/Whale):** Cross-institutional tracking of Earth's longest movements.
2.  **Endemic Echoes:** Exploring species found in only one place (e.g., GBR, Hawaii).
3.  **Pollinator Pathways:** Linking plant occurrences with insect sightings across landscapes.
4.  **Deep Sea Discovery:** Highlighting new species found by NOAA and indexed in GBIF.
5.  **Fossil to Future:** Comparing ancient biodiversity (BHL/NARA) with modern distributions.
6.  **Microbial Marvels:** Life at the extremes (Yellowstone/Hydrothermal Vents).
7.  **The Avian Atlantic:** Tracking bird species across the ocean using GBIF and NASA weather data.
8.  **Botanical Time Capsule:** Using BHL sketches to identify lost flora in modern landscapes.
9.  **Apex Predators:** The role of Sharks, Bears, and Wolves in ecosystem balance.
10. **The Invasive Incursion:** Visualizing the spread of non-native species over time.
